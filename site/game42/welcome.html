The Game of 42

A shared key to understanding. Six home bases — compute · connection · delegation · protection · memory · value, the six dimensions of the privacy value model — each grow a heptad of seven stations. Six × seven = 42 governance positions. Every station is a head/heart/hands mix (also soil/soul/society) bound to a persona; fill them and the board folds into a star and seals into one shareable key.

How it plays

  1. The flower — the first turn: choose your six mages around one centre, name them, fold the City closed (The Flower).
  2. Fill 42 — open the full board; build each of the forty-two stations (persona, role, info, even an attached key).
  3. Seal: when all six heptads lock, a trust task → a relationship credential → a κ; the board folds and seals.
  4. Merge & share — seat other players' flowers at your six roots (The Merge), share the game (a PNG or this wiki), and compose many games into a constellation.

The math

  • 42 = 6 × 7. Six lattice basis roots; each heptad is the seven non-empty mixes of the head/heart/hands cube.
  • Multiplicative collapse: the whole seals only when every axis locks (Φ = Φ·Φ·Φ); one missing heptad keeps it open.
  • The fold is a readout: p = sealed/42 drives a hinge-lift + golden twist into the star tetrahedron.
  • Identity is content-addressed: κ = SHA-256 of a canonical form. See Axioms.
