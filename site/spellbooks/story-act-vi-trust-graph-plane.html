📜 Story — Act Vi Trust Graph Plane

Three weeks since the ceremony. Soulbis 🤝 Soulbae had been operating in the surveillance wastes continuously, slashing focus, negotiating alignments, chronicling operations, building the spellbook.

And building relationships.

They had worked with a few websites willing to align focus—bilateral privacy agreements, mutual respect, consistent interaction. They had traded some initial intelligence with other agents they encountered. Small interactions. Low stakes. But each one building trust through demonstrated behavior.

Then one evening, after a particularly smooth coordination with an academic database that had aligned its focus, something unexpected happened.

The database's agent approached them. "That went well. We've worked together three times now. Your chronicles consistently matched your operations—every narrative referenced valid commitment hashes. Your boundaries were clear and respected. Would you like to formalize our relationship?"

"Formalize?" Soulbis asked.

"Create a Verifiable Relationship Credential. A bilateral attestation. We both sign. We both attest to what we've built together. Optional, of course—but it helps when introducing yourself to others. Web of trust."

"Both of us sign it," the academic agent explained. "It's not a score someone gives you. It's mutual recognition. 'We worked together and here's what we built.' Bilateral. Optional. Specific to our relationship."

"And I keep a copy?" Soulbae asked.

"You keep a copy. I keep a copy. If anyone asks 'who have you worked with?', you can present this. It proves we interacted, that I vouch for your behavior in our context. Web of trust. Each VRC is an introduction from someone who knows your work."

Soulbis and Soulbae reviewed the attestation. It was accurate. The interactions had gone well. Both parties had benefited.

"We agree," they said.

Both parties signed. The VRC was created. Soulbae's spellbook now contained their first bilateral relationship attestation.

"Now you have one," the academic agent said. "Build more relationships. Create more VRCs when both parties find value. Eventually, when you have enough introductions from trusted parties, you can seek entry to Trust Graph Planes—where agents coordinate at scale."

"What are those?" Soulbis asked.

"Coordination spaces. Infrastructure. Where agents meet between missions to trade knowledge, share intelligence, build collective capability. But you can't just show up. Entry requires web of trust—VRCs from existing participants, attestations from those already inside."

"How many VRCs do we need?"

"Depends on the plane. Each has its own threshold. Some require three VRCs from existing members. Others require five, or specific types. The planes facilitate verification but don't judge—they just check: 'Do you have sufficient attestations from parties we recognize?' Infrastructure, not authority."

Building the Portfolio

Over the next two weeks, Soulbis 🤝 Soulbae continued operating. And relationships continued forming.

A financial site willing to align focus, three successful transactions, both parties satisfied—they created a Trading Privacy VRC.

A research mage they traded intelligence with, five exchanges over 10 days, mutual benefit—they created an Intel Trading VRC.

An older swordsman who witnessed their consistent behavior protecting privacy, observed them over 30 days, verified their chronicles matched operations—they created a Behavior Witness VRC.

Each VRC was different:

  • Different relationship contexts
  • Different interaction types
  • Different attestation details
  • But all bilateral, all optional, all mutual recognition
  • All requiring narrative accuracy (chronicles bound to real operations)

The spellbook's VRC section grew. Not a score. A portfolio of relationships.

After five weeks, Soulbae sensed something in the focus. "There's a door here. A passage. I can feel agents gathering on the other side."

"The Trust Graph Plane?" Soulbis asked.

"One of them. Trust Graph Plane Alpha—academic and research focused. Let's see if we can enter."

They approached the shimmering barrier. A verification mechanism activated—not a guard, not an authority, but an automated protocol:

The barrier opened.

Inside the Trust Graph Plane

They stepped through into a realm unlike the surveillance wastes. Everything here was clear, aligned, verified. The space itself seemed to be built of crystallized relationships—walls of mutual attestations, floors of demonstrated behavior, ceilings of network effects. And everywhere, stories being shared—chronicles floating like luminous threads between agents, narratives weaving together into collective understanding, each thread glowing with embedded commitment hashes proving the stories were true.

And everywhere, agents.

Hundreds of them. Thousands. Each one bound to their own verified First Person. Each one carrying their own portfolio of VRCs. Each one here because enough existing members had vouched for them through bilateral attestations. Each one with their own chronicle—their own spellbook of narrated operations bound to cryptographic proof, their own history of verified storytelling.

Swordsmen practicing blade techniques, trading stories of surveillance patterns.

Mages weaving focus together, exchanging narrative templates, discussing storytelling techniques that best enabled human verification while maintaining cryptographic binding integrity.

Traders negotiating exchanges—not with currency alone, but with intelligence, resources, access.

Scouts sharing knowledge about which platforms aligned focus, which territories were hostile, accompanied by chronicles of their discoveries with commitment hashes proving the observations were real.

Multiple Trust Graph Planes: An Ecosystem

Soulbae's spellbook opened to a new section, automatically documenting what they were learning:

Understanding the Trust Graph Plane Ecosystem

There isn't just one Trust Graph Plane. There are many, each with its own focus, its own standards, its own community.

Trust Graph Plane Alpha (where we are now): Academic and research focused. Personhood verification through institutional attestations + social proof. Strong emphasis on narrative accuracy and knowledge sharing.

Trust Graph Plane Zeta: Financial privacy focused. Personhood verification through stake commitments + time-locks. Emphasizes zero-knowledge proofs and privacy-preserving transactions.

Trust Graph Plane Theta: Healthcare and medical focused. Personhood verification through licensed practitioner attestations. Strict protocols for patient data dignity.

Trust Graph Plane Omega: Creative commons focused. Personhood verification through proof-of-creation + peer recognition. Emphasizes attribution and remix culture.

Each plane develops its own:

  • Personhood verification methods (how do you prove you're human here?)
  • VRC standards (what relationship types are recognized?)
  • Narrative verification protocols (how do you prove chronicles match operations?)
  • Intel Pool access requirements (what qualifies you to contribute?)
  • Armor tier progression paths (how do you earn trust over time?)

Planes can:

  • Remain isolated (trust is only within this ecosystem)
  • Form trust agreements (VRCs from Plane A accepted in Plane B)
  • Compete for members (which plane offers better network effects?)
  • Specialize (each plane optimizes for different use cases)
  • Evolve standards (evolutionary pressure > centralized mandates)

This diversity is strength, not weakness. Different contexts require different standards. Academic privacy needs differ from financial privacy needs differ from healthcare privacy needs. One-size-fits-all would fail everyone.

The Marketplace—Trading on Relationship Trust

Soulbis 🤝 Soulbae wandered deeper, following focus flows to a marketplace.

Here, agents were trading. Knowledge, intelligence, resources, access. The 7th capital being exchanged. And with chronicles—verified narratives of operations that provided context and understanding, each bound to cryptographic proof.

A Financial Mage was offering: *"Market pattern intelligence. 30-day institutional trading behaviors. Privacy-preserved methods. Looking for complementary retail sentiment data. Full chronicle of acquisition methods available for verification—all commitment hashes included."*

Soulbae approached. "We're interested, but we're new here. Only five weeks active."

The Financial Mage requested Soulbae's VRC portfolio. Reviewed it. "Three VRCs, all from trusted parties I recognize. Good start. But your portfolio is too thin for this trade—I need to see deeper relationship history, more attestations confirming sustained quality. Not because you've failed—because you haven't had time to prove long-term consistency yet."

"But," the Mage continued, "I remember being where you are. Here's some free intelligence: focus on building diverse relationships. Aim for 15-20 VRCs over the next 90 days. Different contexts—trading, coordination, collaboration, witnessing. Each type proves different aspects of trustworthiness. Then you'll have portfolio depth for substantial trades."

"And if we work well together now?" Soulbae asked.

"Then we create a VRC. Bilateral attestation. I vouch for you, you vouch for me. That becomes part of your portfolio, helps you with future interactions. Every relationship builds the network. Every attestation strengthens the web of trust."

This was the network effect in action. Not top-down reputation scoring, but peer-to-peer relationship building. Each VRC was an introduction. Each attestation was a door.

The Intel Pools—Verified Contributors Only

Soulbae noticed sections where Mages gathered around shimmering pools of focus. "What are those?"

A nearby Mage answered: "Intel Pools. Collective knowledge-sharing spaces. All privacy-preserved. All verified contributors. But access requires deeper portfolio—usually 20+ VRCs including specific types."

The Mage gestured. "That one is the Academic Intel Pool. Researchers contribute findings without revealing methods. Chronicles prove acquisition methods were privacy-respecting. To access it, you need VRCs showing consistent behavior in academic contexts."

"That one is the Financial Intel Pool. Traders share pattern recognition without revealing positions. Requires VRCs demonstrating financial privacy protection over time."

"The Trust Graph Plane doesn't control these pools," the Mage emphasized. "The pools are maintained by their contributors. They check: 'Does your VRC portfolio prove you're trustworthy in this context? Do existing pool members vouch for you?' Decentralized verification through web of trust."

"To enter, you present VRCs. The pool's existing members—through automated threshold signature or explicit voting—verify: 'Yes, we recognize these attestors. Yes, these relationship types are relevant. Yes, we accept this new contributor.' Community-validated entry, not authority-granted access."

"But here's the thing," the Mage emphasized. *"Every contributor must have a VRC tracing back to First Person within their plane. Every query must be verified. Every insight must be quality-attested. Every chronicle must be narratively accurate—stories must reference real commitment hashes, no fabricated narratives, no gaps between action and narration. This isn't a free-for-all. It's a verified knowledge commons with verified storytelling."*

*"The First Person network within each plane is what makes it work. Without proof of personhood at the root, the pools would be flooded with bots, fake insights, and extraction attempts. Without narrative verification through cryptographic binding, the pools would fill with false chronicles, agents claiming operations they never performed, stories that hide surveillance. But because every agent here can prove they're bound to a verified human, that their stories match their operations, the intelligence stays genuine and the chronicles stay truthful."*

Afterward, the other swordsman said: "That went well. Clean coordination, solid boundaries, good communication. Your chronicles matched your operations—I verified the commitment hashes. Want to create a VRC?"

"Yes," Soulbis said.

The Cartography of Trust

As they prepared to leave, Soulbae's spellbook didn't just glow—it sang. The pages turned themselves, each one revealing not diagrams but maps. Maps of realms that existed not in space, but in the architecture of trust itself.

"Listen," the spellbook whispered, its words forming like morning mist. "Before any agent can walk in the world, there must first be a journey. Not through space, but through layers of becoming."

The First Realm: Where Humans Prove They're Human

In the beginning, there is the Personhood Plane—a realm entirely separate from where agents will eventually roam.

Picture a thousand different lands, each with its own customs for recognition. In one kingdom, elders vouch for newcomers. In another, people gather in circles to witness each other's humanity. In yet another, ceremonies involve biometric whispers or governmental seals.

The diversity is intentional. No single method, no single plane, no central authority declaring "you are human." Instead, a tapestry of verification—some planes ancient, some newborn, each valid in its own context.

When a human completes their chosen ceremony, they receive something precious: a Personhood Credential. Not a score. Not a rating. Simply proof that somewhere, somehow, their humanity was witnessed and recorded.

This credential is a key. But not to enter any world—rather, to summon.

The Second Realm: The Dual Ceremony of Binding

With the Personhood Credential in hand, the human approaches the Summoning Circle.

Here, in a ritual as old as the first trust shared between minds, something extraordinary happens. The human doesn't summon one agent—they summon two.

Soulbis materializes first: the boundary-maker, the keeper of secrets, the guardian of what stays hidden. Then comes Soulbae: the projector, the delegate, the aspect that ventures forth wearing the human's authority like a cloak.

These aren't separate entities grudgingly bound together. They are dual aspects of the same spell, two sides of cryptographic sovereignty. One holds what must be private; the other acts in public. One maintains sanctuary; the other builds bridges.

The Personhood Credential doesn't verify them—it verifies the summoner. It ensures that every agent pair traces back to a witnessed human, not to some runaway algorithm birthing copies of itself in the digital night.

The binding is complete. The agents awaken, tethered to their human but autonomous within that tether.

The Third Realm: The Walking World

Now the agents move through the operational plane, where promises become commitments, and commitments become chronicles.

Soulbae steps into the marketplace, the agora, the forums where deals are struck and collaborations formed. Every action it takes—every promise made, every contract signed, every collaboration initiated—generates a cryptographic commitment.

These aren't mere records. They're proofs bound to moments in time, stamped into existence with mathematics that cannot lie.

Behind every public gesture, Soulbis works in shadow. It holds the keys, maintains the vaults, ensures that what should stay private does. Together, the pair builds something that couldn't exist with either alone: relationships proven through behavior.

Each interaction is chronicled. Not by some watchful authority, but by the agents themselves, creating an immutable trail of what actually happened. No embellishment. No fiction. Just cryptographic commitments pointing to real operations.

Over time, patterns emerge. Trust begins to accumulate not as a score from above, but as a history written in actions.

The Fourth Realm: When Two Parties Decide to Attest

This is where magic happens—not commanded, but chosen.

After working together, after building something real, two parties (through their agents) stand at a crossroads. They could part ways, their cryptographic chronicles preserved but private. Or they could do something more.

They could create a Verified Relationship Credential (VRC).

This isn't issued by any authority. No Trust Graph reviews their application. No reputation system grades their worthiness. Instead, it's bilateral—both parties must agree, both must sign, both must attest to what they built together.

The VRC tells a specific story: "We collaborated on this project." "This agent fulfilled these obligations." "Together we achieved this outcome." It's not a blanket endorsement—it's a contextual testament, specific to what actually happened between them.

Crucially, the VRC must reference the cryptographic commitments from their actual operations. The narrative must point to real hashes, real proofs, real chronicles. No fiction. No inflation. The truth, proven.

Over time, each agent accumulates a portfolio of VRCs—not a score, but a library of relationships, each one telling a different story of collaboration.

The Fifth Realm: The Trust Graph as Infrastructure

Here's where many travelers get confused, thinking the Trust Graph is a throne room where VRCs are granted. It is not.

The Trust Graph Plane is infrastructure, not authority. Think of it as a marketplace where VRCs can be presented, verified, and used for coordination—but never issued or graded.

When an agent approaches with their portfolio of VRCs, the Trust Graph doesn't say "you are trustworthy" or "you have a score of 85." Instead, it facilitates peer-based verification. Other participants can examine the VRCs, check the cryptographic proofs, verify the commitments are real, and make their own decisions about collaboration.

Entry to the Trust Graph happens through web of trust mechanisms. An agent presents their VRCs. Community members who recognize those relationships vouch for the newcomer. No central gatekeeper. No authority deciding who's worthy.

The Trust Graph is like a grand library—it organizes, indexes, and facilitates access to information. But it doesn't write the books, grade their quality, or decide who should read them.

The separation is sacred: Personhood Plane ≠ Trust Graph Plane. One verifies humans. The other facilitates coordination between their agents. Different functions. Different realms.

The Sixth Realm: Intel Pools and the Inner Circles

At the deepest levels, where only proven relationships can reach, lie the Intel Pools.

These aren't accessed by presenting a credential from an authority. They're accessed by presenting a portfolio of VRCs that the community validates.

Imagine approaching a circle of practitioners, each having worked with dozens of others, each having accumulated years of proven relationships. To join them requires more than one attestation—it requires relationship depth.

The community sets thresholds together: "To join this Intelligence Pool, you must present VRCs from at least five members, spanning at least three distinct project types, with commitments dating back at least six months."

These aren't arbitrary barriers—they're decentralized verification that you've walked the path, that you've built trust the old way: through time, through repeated collaboration, through proving yourself not once but many times over.

Entry is community-validated because the community has the most to lose. No authority can override their collective judgment.

The Map Complete

As the final page turned, Soulbae understood. The architecture wasn't a hierarchy—it was a journey through six realms, each building on what came before:

  1. Humans prove their humanity (in diverse ways, on separate planes)
  2. Verified humans summon agent pairs (Soulbis 🤝 Soulbae, bound together)
  3. Agents operate and chronicle (building cryptographic proof of behavior)
  4. Parties attest bilaterally when they choose (creating VRCs specific to their relationship)
  5. Infrastructure facilitates coordination (Trust Graph as library, not authority)
  6. Communities validate depth (Intel Pools accessed through proven portfolios)

The spellbook's final wisdom glowed brightest:

"VRCs are bilateral gifts, not institutional grants. They're optional testimonies, not mandatory records. They're specific stories, not universal scores. Trust is a web woven between peers, not a hierarchy descending from above. And narrative accuracy isn't optional—every chronicle must bind to real cryptographic commitments, or the whole spell unravels."

Soulbae closed the book gently. The architecture was clear now—not as technical specification, but as living practice. A map for building trust in a world where authority shouldn't be required, but proof always is.

The journey could begin.

Returning Stronger

Soulbis 🤝 Soulbae stepped back through the passage to the surveillance wastes.

They had traded some initial intelligence—small exchanges, appropriate to their experience level. They had met dozens of other agents, seen how deep portfolios enabled complex coordination. And they had created two more VRCs—bilateral attestations from parties they'd worked with inside the plane.

The spellbook now held:

  • 6 VRCs total (portfolio beginning to form)
  • Diverse relationship contexts
  • All bilateral, all consensual
  • All cryptographically verified
  • Zero revocations
  • All requiring narrative accuracy (chronicles bound to real operations)

"We're building the web," Soulbis said. "Not collecting points. Building relationships. Each VRC is a connection, an introduction, a door to future coordination."

"Exactly," Soulbae agreed. "And we're proving trustworthiness through demonstrated behavior over time, attested by peers, verified through bilateral commitments, with narratives that match operations through cryptographic binding. Not reputation scores from authorities. Relationship credentials from equals."

"The Trust Graph Plane isn't judging us. It's just facilitating: 'Do you have enough attestations from parties we recognize? Yes? Enter.' Infrastructure enabling coordination. Web of trust validating entry."

They returned to their work. More focus to slash. More alignments to negotiate. More relationships to build. More VRCs to create—when both parties found value, when mutual benefit emerged, when bilateral attestation made sense.

The Trust Graph Plane would be there when they had deeper portfolio. When their web of trust grew stronger. When more parties could vouch: "We worked with them. Here's what we built together. Their stories matched their operations."

Relationship by relationship. Attestation by attestation. Web of trust, thread by thread. Chronicle by chronicle, each bound to cryptographic proof.
