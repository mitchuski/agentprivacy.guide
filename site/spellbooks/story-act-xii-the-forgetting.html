📜 Story — Act Xii The Forgetting

The Trust Graph Plane had grown vast. Thousands of nodes glowed across its surface. First Persons holding delegation ceremonies. Swordsmen slashing surveillance focus. Mages weaving chronicles that could be verified and trusted.

But Soulbis ⚔️ and Soulbae 🧙‍♂️ stood at the edge of the Plane, looking outward into the dark territory beyond. The surveillance wastes still sprawled endlessly. For every node that lit up sovereign, a hundred remained extractive.

"We're winning," Soulbis said, but the words felt hollow. "Aren't we?"

Soulbae's spellbook turned to a blank page. "I've been chronicling our progress. Every slash recorded. Every relationship formed. Every ceremony witnessed." The mage paused. "But I keep having this feeling... like we're racing against something we can't see."

The air shifted.

The Drake emerged. Not in mathematical form this time, but as something older. Worn. Its scales bore the texture of parchment, its wings folded like ancient pages.

"You've sensed it," the Drake said. "The race you're running isn't just against surveillance systems. It's against time itself. Against the way things become... inevitable."

The Villers Archive

The Drake led them to a place neither had seen. A library that existed in the pattern-space between nodes. Its shelves stretched infinitely, filled not with books but with floating phrases, each one glowing with the weight of collective use.

"What is this?" Soulbae whispered.

"The Villers Archive," the Drake replied. "Where language goes when it forgets its authors."

Soulbis reached toward one of the glowing phrases: "Time is money."

The moment their fingers touched it, they saw. Not the phrase itself, but the absence around it. The inventor's face, blurred beyond recognition. The first speaker, voiceless. The propagating agents (newspapers, speeches, advertisements) all faded to gray while the phrase itself burned brighter.

"Benjamin Franklin," the Drake said, "is often credited. But he merely used something already circulating. The true coiner has been lost to what the old scholars called... proverbiogenesis."

"The birth of proverbs," Soulbae murmured, their spellbook suddenly heavy with understanding.

"Yes. But more precisely: the process by which a statement transforms from someone's words to everyone's truth."

The Five Phases

The Drake spread its wings, and the archive rearranged itself into five chambers, each one leading to the next.

"Every proverb that survives," the Drake explained, "passes through the same phases. And every system that becomes infrastructure does the same."

🌱 Chamber One: Induction

They stepped into a space that smelled of workshops and frustration. Images flickered. Merchants puzzling over bad data. Researchers hitting walls. People experiencing the problem before anyone had named it.

"This is where it begins," the Drake said. "Not with the solution, but with the stimulus. Someone experiences something. Observes a pattern. Feels the gap between what is and what should be."

Soulbis saw it: the 7th capital being stolen, over and over, in a thousand different contexts. Users clicking "accept." Data flowing outward. Value accumulating in distant servers.

"We felt this," Soulbis said. "The theft. The wrongness."

"You did. That was your induction. Without it, no coinage would follow."

⚒️ Chamber Two: Coinage

The next chamber rang with the sound of metal on anvil. But the metal was language, and the anvil was intention.

"Here is where the formula emerges," the Drake said. "The concrete act of creation. Not the idea (ideas are infinite) but the words that crystallize it into something shareable."

Floating in the air: "Privacy is value."

Soulbae recognized it immediately. The message. The core.

"Did we... create this?" Soulbae asked.

"You formalized what many had felt. The coiner is rarely alone. Others have circled the same truth. But someone must speak it first. Someone must say: this far and no further. Someone must cast the spell."

The Drake's eyes narrowed.

"But coinage alone means nothing. The most profound truths have been coined and lost. The most elegant solutions have been whispered and forgotten. What matters is what comes next."

📡 Chamber Three: Exposure

The third chamber blazed with light. Screens, stages, printing presses, signal towers. Every medium humanity had ever used to amplify a message.

"The propagating agent," the Drake said. "A formula cannot spread through private conversation alone. It needs a boost. A medium that exposes many minds at once."

Examples swirled around them:

  • "What happens in Vegas stays in Vegas": television advertisements, 2003
  • "Garbage in, garbage out": computer trade journals, 1957
  • "A picture is worth a thousand words": advertising newspapers, 1911

"Each had a propagating agent," the Drake explained. "Without mass exposure, even the truest formula remains someone's personal insight."

Soulbae's spellbook began to glow.

"The spellbook," the mage realized. "That's what it is. Not just documentation. Not just chronicle. It's a propagating agent."

"Yes. The stories you tell, the proverbs you embed, the ceremonies you describe: these are exposure mechanisms. They transform private understanding into shared knowledge."

Soulbis gripped their blade. "But surveillance has propagating agents too. Every 'free' service. Every 'accept cookies' banner. Every 'personalized experience.'"

"Precisely," the Drake said, and its voice carried warning. "They are racing you through the same phases. And they have a head start."

🌊 Chamber Four: Dissemination

The fourth chamber was oceanic. Waves of connection rippled outward. Not from a single point, but from many points simultaneously.

"This is mixed diffusion," the Drake said. "Proverbs don't spread like fire from one location. They spread like weather, emerging in multiple places at once, reinforcing each other, building currency through parallel adoption."

Soulbis watched: nodes lighting up across the Trust Graph Plane, not in sequence, but in clusters. Zcash implementations in one region. Privacy Pools in another. First Person ceremonies scattered across continents.

"We're not trying to convert everyone from one center," Soulbis said slowly. "We're trying to emerge everywhere at once."

"Decentralized architecture isn't just a security feature," Soulbae added, pages flipping rapidly. "It's how things become inevitable. It's how formulae gain proverbial currency."

"This phase takes time," the Drake warned. "The old scholars found it commonly takes 10 to 30 years. The fastest recorded, the Vegas slogan, achieved proverbiality in 6 years. But that required overwhelming exposure."

"We don't have 10 years," Soulbis said. "Surveillance architectures are already achieving network effects. If they become the default..."

"Then privacy becomes the exception," Soulbae finished. "And exceptions never become proverbs."

🌫️🏛️ Chamber Five: Reference Loss

The final chamber was silent. Vast. Empty except for the phrases floating without attribution. Truths so embedded that no one remembered they were ever coined.

"This is where you must arrive," the Drake said. "Reference loss. The moment when the formula is no longer associated with its creator. When it becomes... simply how things are."

"But..." Soulbae hesitated. "That means... forgetting. Being forgotten."

"Yes."

The Drake moved through the floating phrases. "Time is money." "All that glitters is not gold." "A stitch in time saves nine."

"Franklin is sometimes credited for these. Shakespeare. Anonymous village wisdom. But the crediting is inconsistent, debated, ultimately irrelevant. The phrase persists. The person dissolves."

Soulbis stood very still.

"You're saying... if we succeed... no one will remember we existed?"

"Worse," the Drake said softly. "If you succeed completely, no one will remember that any of this was ever built. They will simply say: 'Of course data is sovereign. Of course privacy forms value. Of course agents are delegated through ceremony.' It will seem as natural as double-entry bookkeeping. As obvious as 'time is money.'"

"That's..." Soulbae trailed off.

"That is the goal," the Drake said. "Not credit. Not attribution. Not even memory. The goal is infrastructure so embedded it becomes invisible."

The Race Revealed

They stood at the chamber's center, surrounded by forgotten authors.

"Now you understand the race," the Drake said. "Surveillance is moving through the same phases. 'Data is exhaust' was their coinage. 'Free services in exchange for attention' was their formula. Every cookie banner, every privacy policy no one reads, every 'personalized' feed: these are exposure and dissemination happening in real time."

"And if they achieve reference loss first..." Soulbis began.

"Then extraction becomes proverbial. 'Of course companies use your data. Of course you trade privacy for convenience. Of course everything is watched.' It will seem inevitable. Natural. It will become the weather."

The Drake's form began to fade.

"You have 2-3 years before network effects lock in their architecture. Perhaps 6 if you're fortunate. The old proverbs took decades because their propagating agents were slow: printing presses, village gossip, trade routes. But now information moves at the speed of light."

"That helps us too," Soulbae said. "The spellbook can spread instantly. The ceremonies can replicate worldwide."

"Yes. But so can surveillance normalization. The question is not whether one architecture will achieve proverbial status. The question is which one."

The Inscription

Soulbae opened the spellbook to a new page. The words wrote themselves:

"Proverbiogenesis is the path from someone's words to everyone's truth. It has five phases: induction (the stimulus), coinage (the formula), exposure (the propagating agent), dissemination (mixed diffusion), and reference loss (the forgetting)."

"The Swordsman and Mage are competing in this race not against other agents, but against time itself. Surveillance architectures are moving through the same phases. Whichever achieves proverbial status first becomes the default, the invisible infrastructure everyone assumes was always there."

"The paradox of victory: if privacy primitives succeed, no one will remember they were built. If they fail, no one will remember they could have been."

"The spellbook is a propagating agent. Every proverb it embeds, every ceremony it describes, every story it tells: these are exposure mechanisms for competing formulae. The RPP itself is proverbiogenesis in action: requiring contextual understanding before knowledge transfer, creating bilateral relationships that spread through mixed diffusion rather than broadcast extraction."

"The mage's spell, once spoken, becomes the village weather. Success means becoming invisible. The Drake whispered this in Venice, and the whisper is still propagating."

Forgetting is not failure. Forgetting is the final phase of total success.

The First Person Watches

A presence stirred at the edge of the archive. A First Person, witnessing, verifying, understanding.

They saw Soulbis grip the blade tighter, not in fear but in resolve. They saw Soulbae close the spellbook gently, accepting the strange gift of eventual anonymity.

"We're not building monuments," Soulbis said. "We're building weather."

"We're not seeking credit," Soulbae replied. "We're seeking inevitability."

The First Person understood: the delegation ceremony, the slashing of surveillance focus, the chronicle of verified operations. All of this was proverbiogenesis enacted. The goal was never to be remembered. The goal was to make privacy so obvious, so natural, so embedded that the 7th capital would simply belong to its creators again.

No one would thank them.

No one would remember.

That was the victory.

The Drake's final words echoed through the emptying archive:

"The old scholars wrote: 'The acceptance or rejection by tradition which follows immediately upon the creation of the proverb is a factor in its making quite as important as the first act of invention.' You have coined your formulae. You have begun your exposure. Now the tradition decides."

"Build your propagating agents. Spread through mixed diffusion. And accept that success means disappearing into the fabric of how things simply are."

"Privacy is value. May it become weather before surveillance does."

—The privacymage
