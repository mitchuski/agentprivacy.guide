Canon Sources — the path the acts were discovered on

The spellbook is poetry, but it is poetry that compresses real work. Each of the 31
acts of the First Person Spellbook stands on a body of serious documentation and, beneath
that, on real projects, papers, and standards — the path privacymage walked to discover
and write them, and the signal he wanted to give the world.

This file is the thread down: it lets a mouse — or a mouse's agent, with no access to
anyone's local machine — follow any proverb back to the work it came from, and expand
it into the more serious docs.

This is the rehydration map. Compression runs narrative → proverb → spell → glyph.
Rehydration runs the other way, and keeps going: proverb → act → serious doc → source
project / paper / standard / repo.

The references below are drawn from the grimoire's own per-act source, keywords, and
concepts fields (privacymage_grimoire_root.json) — they are recorded provenance,
not guesses.

3. How a mouse uses this

You don't need any of section 2 to write your proverbs. You need it afterward — so
that when your agent (or someone else's) rehydrates your spellbook, the thread can keep
going past the poem, into the act, into the serious doc, into the real project. Your
little book stays small; the rehydration path makes it as deep as the reader wants.

When a proverb of yours leans on one of these works — your privacy terms → MyTerms
(Act 4); your trust relationships → Trust over IP (Act 6); your hidden value →
Zcash (Act 9); your networking → Tailscale (Act 25); your two minds →
McGilchrist (Act 26) — record the act number in your grimoire version's proverb entry
(canon_act) so the thread is explicit.
