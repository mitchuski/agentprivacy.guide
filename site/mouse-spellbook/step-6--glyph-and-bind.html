Step 6 — Glyph + bind your chapbook

Give each proverb a body. Then bind them.

6a — Draw each spell as an ASCII glyph (Template — ASCII Glyph): a small
monospace scene (heavy chars = ink/subject; light chars = silver/atmosphere) with 2–4
sparkle marks — + a positive promise ("I will"), - a negative one ("I will not")
— and a polarity ratio beneath. Write them into your-folder/glyphs.md; copy each
glyph's name back into the matching proverb's glyph_ref.

6b — Bind the chapbook (Template — Little Book) into
your-folder/spellbook.md: title page + your sigil; an epigraph (your one true thing);
one page per proverb (glyph, proverb, poem, spell beneath); a colophon — "just another
mouse, <name>" + your reason + your seal.

→ Gate G5 (opens) — the facilitator chronicles the two bound chapbooks.
