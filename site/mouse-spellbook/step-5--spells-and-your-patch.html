Step 5 — Sync to the spell-emoji string + record your personal patch

This is the overlap. This is how your book speaks the canon's tongue.

For each proverb, write a spell from The Spell-Emoji Legend into
your-folder/spells.md. Lay the forces in order of becoming (⚔️ refuse · 🧙 give ·
⊥ gap · △ proof · 🌀 manifold), joined by connectors (→ ← ║ × ∴), closing with
the reason (😊) where it fits.

The overlap check: every symbol in a spell must be in the shared legend — or declared
in your patch's legend.additions with its meaning. Named, deliberate, never accidental.

Record your personal patch. Copy Template — Grimoire Version (JSON) into
your-folder/grimoire_<name>_v0_1.json and fill it: your first_person, your acts[]
(one line per act, distilled from your narrative), your proverbs[] (proverb + spell +
glyph_ref + optional canon_act), your legend.additions, your signature. It reads
the canon as a root (derived_from); it never edits it. You pin it when ready.

Bridge it to your own work (optional · private). Using Template — Context Bridge,
map the acts that resonate to the real projects and directories on your machine →
your-folder/context-bridge.md (and the patch's local_context). This is the Swordsman's
domain: it stays local, is stripped before any public pin, and never enters the Mouse Book.
It lets your own agent ground a proverb in the actual work it concerns.

→ Gate G4 — the facilitator chronicles the spells, the overlap-check, and the patch.
