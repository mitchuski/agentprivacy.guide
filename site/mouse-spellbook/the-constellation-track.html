The Constellation Track — how a mouse's constellation is tracked in the spellweb

A ceremony track artefact for the Holon Hitchhikers. First Person Spellbook only.

The last page of the spellbook says it plainly:

same stars hang in every sky. the lines between them are yours alone.
you draw yours. that is the meaning.

So the 31 acts are the fixed stars — the same sky every mouse receives. A mouse's
constellation is the set of acts that rang true for them and the lines they draw
between them — which acts connect, for them. No two mice draw the same lines; the
drawing is the meaning. The Constellation Track is that drawing, made into an artefact
that can be tracked in the spellweb (spellweb.ai), the shared knowledge-graph substrate.

This is the new ceremony of the day. Walking in, a mouse becomes a Holon Hitchhiker;
their constellation track is their first record in the stream.

The ceremony: Walk · Light · Track

Matching the spellweb's ceremony grammar (Chart Shop Hold·Compare·Map; Familiars
Run·Evoke·Spawn):

  1. Walk — go through the 31 acts (this is Step 3 of the workshop, the retelling).
  2. Light — mark each act that rings true for you. The lit acts are your stars.
  3. Track — draw the lines between acts that rhyme for you, name each line, and bind
    the whole as a constellation track artefact you can carry into the graph.

How it tracks into the spellweb (the real vocabulary)

The spellweb already holds the machinery — the artefact reuses it, edits nothing:

  • The stars are act nodes. The 31 First Person acts already live in the graph with
    spellbook: first_person. Each may inhabit a vertex on the 64-lattice (the geometric
    ground). You do not create the stars; you light the ones already there.
  • Your lines are edges in the deviation layer. The lines you draw between acts are
    your appended data — stored the way the spellweb stores a Sovereign's own edges
    (spellweb-user-edges / spellweb-constellations), relates_to between two act nodes,
    never written into the canon. (Deviation layer, 2026-05-11: "the user's appended data on
    top of the universe.")
  • The artefact node is a held constellation. The spellweb has an EntityKind: 'held'
    — "a constellation kept in suspension under the Φ-gap · may release to Bonfire/Weavers/sea
    or stay private."     Your track imports as one: an artefact anchored to the acts it lit, with
    traced_through (→ the lit acts/vertices, the ceremony's evidence) and anchors_to (→ the
    First Person Spellbook root).
  • It is witnessed metadata-only — the Φ-gap holds. You share the shape, not the
    drawing: a bearer_consent_token, the count of lit stars, the stratum summary, the line
    count — never the raw lines or what each act means to you. (Mirrors the held-constellation
    witness rule: the underlying vertices stay in the bearer's keeping.) Patterns can be
    copied; choosing cannot be harvested.

Scope. This uses the spellweb's shared substrate and its first_person filter, and
borrows the held / Φ-gap pattern. It does not make you a citizen of the City of
Mages (the Second Person world) — that is nearby and linked, not today's subject.

The Holon (why this is a Holon Hitchhikers artefact)

Two mice receive the same 31 stars and draw different lines. Lay both constellations over
the one sky and their overlap — the acts you both lit, the lines you both drew — is the
smallest Holon: two wholes making a larger whole. This is the same proof the Mouse Book
makes, drawn in stars instead of poems. The Constellation Track is the artefact each mouse
carries; the overlap is what the Holon Hitchhikers witness together.

What you produce

Template — Constellation Track → your-folder/constellation-track.md:

  • the private drawing (your lit acts + your named lines) — stays on your machine, or
    pins only when you choose;
  • the witness block (metadata-only) — the shape you let others track;
  • a one-line tracking note for an agent: import as a held constellation, anchor to the
    first-person acts, carry the witness block, honour the Φ-gap.

the same stars. your own lines.
