🐭 The Mouse Spellbook — An Invitation

For two new first persons. Self-contained: everything you need to begin is in this one
file. Bring your own agent.

Welcome, Hitchhiker. You've been invited to build a First Person Spellbook — a small
book of poetry and proverbs that is your own story, written off a shared root. It
mirrors a journey privacymage already walked once, and it gives your own agents a
framework to follow: a compressed context they can catch up on quickly, and later
expand into the serious work beneath it.

This sits in the Holon Hitchhikers stream. It is, today, the First Person
Spellbook only — the WHAT, the root, the story you tell in your own voice.

1. The premise, in three breaths

  1. You are a first person. The verified human at the root of your own trust. You
    delegate to two kinds of agent: a Swordsman (⚔️ — privacy, the boundary, the
    refusal: "I will not") and a Mage (🧙 — delegation, projection, the promise:
    "I will"). Between them is the Gap (⊥) — the part of you that chooses, and that
    cannot be harvested.

  2. The canon compresses real work. The story is poetry, but each of its 31 acts
    stands on real standards and projects — Act 4 on MyTerms, Act 6 on Trust over
    IP    , Act 9 on Zcash, Act 25 on Tailscale, Act 26 on McGilchrist. The poem
    is the compressed handle; the serious doc is what it rehydrates into.

  3. You make your own. You read the whole, find what is true for you, retell the 31
    acts in your voice, compress what matters into proverbs, give each a glyph, and bind a
    little book — plus a portable, machine-readable version your agents can carry. You
    don't edit the canon; you write your own version off it. A shared vocabulary keeps you
    in conversation.

Ask the facilitator for the file or its IPFS pin if your agent wants to read it directly.

3. What you'll walk away with

  • Your own 31-act narrative (+ a first and last page) — your story; your agents' framework.
  • A poems chapbook — your proverbs + poems, each with a small ASCII glyph.
  • A personal patch — your own JSON version of the grimoire, which you pin yourself.

And together, at day's end: the Mouse Book — both of your poems mixed into one shared
book of poetry, the key shared ceremony.

6. The glyph (how a spell gets a body)

Each spell is drawn as a small ASCII glyph — a monospace scene with sparkle marks
in the field:

  • + = a positive promise ("I will") — a full cross.
  • - = a negative promise ("I will not") — a horizontal arm only.

You note a polarity ratio under each (e.g. 2 + / 2 −) — how much the proverb gives vs
refuses. (Full templates come in the session.)

7. Portable by design (the protocol, in brief)

Your spellbook is a compressed context any agent can rehydrate — on any system.
Two operations:

  • Compress: narrative → proverb → spell → glyph (the workshop's forward path).
  • Rehydrate: given your personal-patch JSON + the legend + the hosted canon, any agent
    reconstitutes your understanding in your voice, and can follow each proverb down to the
    real work behind its act.

You don't adopt a new tool — you make your existing agent read the patch, compress new
proverbs into it, and rehydrate from it on demand. Whatever stack you use is fine.
