Template — Wiki Naming Convention (the mouse's distribution standard)

When your book leaves your folder and becomes a wiki — pages an agent or a stranger can
walk — the directories, files, and page names need to be predictable. A predictable
name is a rehydration handle: it lets any agent, with no access to your machine, follow a
link from a proverb to its act to the serious doc beneath (Canon Sources).

This is the place where you build that convention and write your distribution
instructions. There is a shared baseline below so every mouse's wiki reads the same
way; you may adopt it as-is, or override any line — but write down what you chose, so
your agents (and the other mice) can follow it.

Copy this file into your folder as wiki-naming.md and fill the two Your instructions
sections. Leave the baseline intact for reference, or strike through what you change.

A. The baseline standard (shared — adopt or override)

1. One root, named for you.
Your wiki root is your mouse-name as a slug: mouse-<name>-spellbook/
(slug rule below). Everything distributes under it.

2. The slug rule (paths are ascii; titles keep the magic).

  • lowercase · words joined by single hyphens · ascii-folded (é → e).
  • no emoji, spaces, or · in a path or filename — sigils and spells live in page
    titles     and content, never in the slug. (🌙 is a title; the-tide is its slug.)
  • a slug is stable: once published, it does not change (rename = a redirect, not an edit).

3. The folder system (mirrors your workshop folder).

Folder Wiki section Holds
narrative/ The 31 Acts 00-first-page, act-01-<slug> … act-31-<slug>, 99-last-page
proverbs/ The Proverbs one page per proverb: proverb-<NN>-<slug>
glyphs/ The Glyphs one page per ascii glyph: glyph-<NN>-<slug>
chapbook/ The Chapbook the bound little book (single page or one-per-spread)
patch/ The Patch your grimoire_<name>_v0_1.json + a human page that renders it
legend/ The Legend your legend.additions (the symbols you coined)

4. The act filename form (matches the canon's own).
act-<NN>-<roman>-<slug> — e.g. act-01-i-venice, act-31-xxxi-the-first-delegation.
Zero-padded arabic for sort order; roman for the canon link; slug for the human.
First/last pages: 00-first-page, 99-last-page.

5. Page titles (where the magic lives).
The title is the human name with sigils and spells; the slug is its ascii reduction.
Title: 🌙 Act I — The Tide → slug: act-01-i-the-tide.

6. Cross-links (the [ ] hand).
Link in the spellbook's own bracket form, by slug, relative:
[act-04-iv-the-blade-alone], [proverb-02-what-i-keep].
Every proverb page links down to its canon_act and out to its serious source
(Canon Sources) — that is the rehydration thread made navigable.

7. The manifest (one machine-readable index).
MANIFEST.json at the wiki root maps every page: { slug, title, kind, canon_act, source, spell }. This is what an agent reads first to walk the whole wiki without
guessing names. Generated, not hand-kept.

C. Your instructions — distribution

Where this wiki goes, and how an agent reaches it with no machine in the loop.

Distribution target(s): <e.g. a public wiki host · GitHub wiki · IPFS dir · own site>
Canonical URL / CID:    <filled when you publish; mirrors patch.pinning.pin_cid>
Index entry point:      <root>/MANIFEST.json  +  <root>/README
Rehydration pointer:    point agents at the shared legend + the hosted canon
                        (agentprivacy.ai · spellweb.ai · soulbis.com) per your patch's
                        derived_from.hosted, then at this wiki's MANIFEST.
Update rule:            slugs are stable; new pages are added, never renamed in place.

The rule above the rules: you publish your own wiki under your own names. The
baseline only keeps the mice legible to each other — it is a shared tongue for paths, the
way the spell-emoji legend is a shared tongue for meaning. Read the standard; write your own.

just another mouse, naming their own rooms.
