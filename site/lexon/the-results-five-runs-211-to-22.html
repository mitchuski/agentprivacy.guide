📉 The Results — Five Runs, 211 To 22

The ladder from 211 to 22, what crossed into constrained language, the two
kills, and the honest residue.

The ladder

The metric is coverage-debt: how many of the 211 frozen canon terms are
not yet expressed as gate-passing Lexon. It started at 211 and only ever
falls when a genuinely general expression survives the full gate.

stage debt what landed
census freeze 211 the deterministic 211-term census
seed-fold-1 201 10 hand-authored seed entries, honestly labelled
lexr1 181 4 recipes validated; the core architecture and promise theory
lexr2 152 the quantitative register; the first mirage (a false self-certification)
lexr3 123 the mutation-hardened gate; the "spell" lens for agent-behavior terms
the constitution 123 TRUSTS + seats as 13 verified spells (no metric move: honest)
lexr4 64 eleven recipes; the consent stack; the first structural kill
numeric layer 64 a checker extension unlocking the formula tail (no metric move yet)
lexr5 22 the formula and story tails; 90 percent of the census covered

Five loop runs, twenty-nine validated levers, two structural kills, zero
unverified entries at any fold. Every number resolves to frontier.json.

What crossed into constrained language

The 189 covered terms are, by domain:

  • The core architecture — First Person, Sovereignty, the Privacy-Delegation
    Paradox, the Gap, the two agents, the Superagent, the Separation Theorem,
    the Six Dimensions of Sovereignty.
  • Promise theory, complete — the whole glossary block (Autonomy Axiom
    through Valency) and all eight promise-theory concept-mapping rows. The
    theory the model rests on is fully expressed.
  • The formal core — Entropy, Mutual Information, the Reconstruction
    Ceiling and Error Floor, the Separation Matrix, the three-axis / three-Phi
    separation formalism, Tetrahedral Sovereignty, the Four Forces.
  • The consent and standards stack, complete — the entire IEEE 7012 /
    MyTerms cluster (First Party, Agreement, Contract, Entity, Proposer, the
    Agreement Taxonomy, Customer Commons) plus TSP, x402, ERC-8004, ERC-7812.
  • The relationship, ceremony, mana, and city layers — VRC, trust tiers,
    the Guardian, the ceremony system, the mana economy, City of Mages
    governance, the V5 value calculus.
  • The security and temporal narratives — the Quantum Threshold, the 2D
    Fortress, the 62-Lap Theorem, the Amnesia Protocol, the Temporal Thesis.
  • The formula and geometry tail — Conditional Independence, ZKP, the
    holographic and toroidal geometry, BRAID — expressible once a numeric
    layer     (number types, initial literals, comparative predicates, all attested
    from Diedrich's own 0.7-era sample) was added to the grammar.
  • The cosmological and story terms that name structure the model owns —
    Yggdrasil, the Theia Partition, the thirteen named vertices — expressible via
    a universal cast rule (a canon section naming no actor takes the First
    Person as holder; no role is ever invented).
  • The harness's own constitution — TRUSTS T1-T6 and the seven seats, as
    thirteen verified spells.

The two kills, filed at win-prominence

A research instrument earns trust by how it handles failure. Two levers were
killed, each recorded as prominently as any win, each with a re-open
condition:

  • K-2 — a recipe for agent-behavior terms that honestly could not express
    a class of "zero-party" canon terms (sections that name no acting party).
    Its own rule forbade inventing a role to fill the gap, and it cited a
    previous run's refusal as binding precedent. It died on that honesty, plus
    one misplaced em dash. Its re-open condition — a "universal cast rule" —
    was exactly what the next run built, and the kill was reopened and resolved.
  • K-3 — mechanically perfect (every claim passed), killed on a single em
    dash in a term field. But its ground had already been covered by earlier
    levers in the same run, so nothing was lost. A clean example of the rule: a
    result that violates the hard constraint is not a result at any score.

Two ideas the runs proved out

  • A claim can be proven distinguishable from its opposite (the mutation
    probe). This is what makes privacy-as-architecture checkable rather than
    asserted; it is the project's central technical result.
  • Convergence is a real risk, and disjoint territories dissolve it. By
    lexr4 the three proposal "lenses" had quietly become one method, so their
    metrics double-counted. lexr5 solved it structurally: it partitioned the
    remaining terms into non-overlapping territories, one per lens, so the
    metric became additive with no arbitration. A methodological lesson with
    general reach.

The residue: 22 terms, and why they remain

  • 16 ordinary terms the territory sweeps did not reach in the runs that
    fit before the session limits. One more pass should close most; anything
    that resists a full dry run becomes honest, named, permanent residue.
  • 6 conjecture-register terms deliberately reserved. Their canon titles
    carry conjecture numbers and confidence percentages, and no automated seat
    is permitted to write those (the conjecture register is operator-write-only,
    a standing rule). Expressing them needs a human naming convention first.

What "covered" does and does not mean

Two honest caveats, stated plainly:

  • Covered means the census, and the census is the canon's index, not its
    body.     The 211 terms were frozen from the glossary headings and the
    promise-theory mapping rows. The conjecture register, the full model
    equations, the papers, and the ten Tomes of story are not censused. Coverage
    is over the index.
  • Per term, covered means the verified structure, not the substance. Each
    entry carries one machine-checked structural relation and names what it
    omits. The argument of the model is in constrained language; the deep
    mathematical frontier and the myth are, deliberately, left home.
