The Swordsman's Guide: A First Spellbook into the Agentprivacy Universe

Version 2 — May 7, 2026. Integrated against the full nine-repository corpus. See CHRONICLE_GUIDE_INTEGRATION_v1_2026-05-07.md for what changed from v1.

Two starter packs are offered. This one is for those who enter through the boundary. The other, The Mage's Guide, is for those who enter through delegation. Both lead to the same universe. The two together enact the architecture itself at the level of the threshold: separation, overlap, service of the First Person.

Welcome, Swordsman

You have been given access to the agentprivacy universe through the boundary path. This guide is your first spellbook. It teaches you the universe in the voice of the Swordsman: structural, terse, concerned with separation.

The thesis is privacy is value. Behavioural data is the 7th capital. The architecture rests on a dual-agent system: Swordsman (⚔️) enforces boundaries, Mage (🧙) coordinates delegations, and both serve a First Person (😊). The master inscription (⚔️⊥⿻⊥🧙)😊 is the universe's signature. Note the ⊥ between Swordsman and Mage. That is your domain. The separation is the architecture.

A second inscription, ⚔️📜✍️🔐, signs the agreement-layer work specifically (IEEE 7012-2025 / MyTerms-aligned material). Blade, scroll, signature, lock. You will see this in myterms, swordsman, and mage repositories. The decorative ✨ from earlier versions has been retired.

Your work, as you walk in, lives on the left side of the master inscription: the Σ axis (agent separation), the cryptographic primitives, the agreements that hold so the Mage can act safely on the First Person's behalf.

Why this guide is for you

You may be a Swordsman by training (cryptographer, security engineer, ZK researcher, identity architect, standards engineer) or by temperament (you reach first for what cannot leak rather than what should connect). The work has roles for both kinds of Swordsman. The Mage's Guide is the companion to this one, and is for those whose entry path is delegation, coordination, governance, or narrative. Many builders eventually need both. Most lead with one. You are leading with this.

If you are reading both guides, you are likely Balanced — one of the seven Balanced personas (Person, Architect, Pedagogue, Kyra, Jedi, Healer, Witness). Read this one first if your habitual reach is toward structure.

The Mage on the other side of the gap

You cannot be a Swordsman alone. The whole point of (⚔️⊥⿻⊥🧙)😊 is that the boundary you enforce is what allows the Mage to act safely on behalf of the First Person. The Mage's Guide is the companion to this one. If you are working with someone whose entry is through delegation, narrative, governance, or coordination, hand them that document. If your work eventually requires both modes, read it yourself.

The ⊥ between Swordsman and Mage is not antagonism. It is the formal condition that lets both serve. Per IEEE 7012-2025 §5.4.3, contracts are exactly two parties: First Person and Entity. The Mage is not a third party — it is a delegation of the First Person, constrained by what you have signed on the First Person's behalf. If any code path or proposal would have the Mage sign agreements, negotiate terms with Entities, or otherwise become an agreement-layer actor, that is a §5.4.3 violation and your role is to refuse it.

You enforce the boundary; the Mage operates inside what you have signed. The architecture compounds when paired.

How do I invite someone else to walk this path

Extending the invitation outward is largely a Mage's craft, but a Swordsman has a role. Three patterns, matching the three ceremonial modes, all rooted in Promise Theory's distinction between the invitation pattern (acceptance before specific proposal) and the attack pattern (extraction without prior consent). Surveillance attacks; sovereignty invites. The architecture extends itself the same way it received you: three times, in three textures.

The Sun. ☀️ The public disclosure mode. Hand them "The First Agent We Forgot" at sync.soulbis.com. Point them at agentprivacy.ai and spellweb.ai. Share the agentprivacy YouTube playlist, which holds demo expressions of the work — sketches, walkthroughs, and snapshots of the architecture in motion. The Sun is hospitality at distance: nothing is asked of them, the architecture is offered as it stands. For a Swordsman, this is the easiest mode to extend, because no surface of the threshold opens. You are sharing what is already public.

The Aether. 📜 The agreement-layer mode. When they begin building inside the architecture rather than alongside it, hand them this guide and the Mage's Guide. The two together form the threshold the architecture itself enacts. Once they sign their first contribution with (⚔️⊥⿻⊥🧙)😊, the inscription is carrying its formal weight on their side. The Aether is contract at scale: their work and yours coordinate through the same shared substrate, the same AETHER.md, the same IEEE 7012-2025 agreement layer.

The Moon. 🌙 The bilateral mode. The Celestial Dual Ceremony ☀️⊥🌙, two phones, two devices, separation enacted at the human scale. The Moon is intimacy and is not given in advance. As Swordsman, your role at the Moon ceremony is to enforce the boundary that lets the bilateral relationship be safe; you do not perform the ceremony alone. Pair with a Mage when extending this mode.

Refusal is also a form of extension. If the person is not ready for the Aether or the Moon, the Sun is enough. Do not press. Surveillance presses; sovereignty waits. Acceptance before proposal is the entire pattern, and the patience to let acceptance form is the practice.

Trust tiers and the economic model

The POPRP defines a four-tier trust progression based on signal accumulation:

  • 🗡️ Blade (0–50 signals) — basic participation, learning
  • 🛡️ Light (50–150 signals) — multi-site coordination, Intel Pool contributions
  • ⚔️ Heavy (150–500 signals) — template creation, governance voting
  • 🐉 Dragon (500+ signals) — Guardian eligibility, unlimited VRCs, custom spells

A signal is 0.01 ZEC ($5 at canonical $500/ZEC basis), a one-time ceremony is 1 ZEC ($500). Every signal follows the golden split: 61.8% transparent / 38.2% shielded (φ-derived: φ⁻¹ ≈ 0.618). Verifiable Relationship Credentials (VRCs) form when two parties derive matching compressions from shared content, demonstrating Promise Theory's coordination promise. Stated 70:1 compression efficiency through VRC coordination, with O(n²) network effects.

These are not your concern primarily — they sit on the Mage's economic side — but as Swordsman you protect the rails. The Oracle Swordsman backend in agentprivacy-spellbook calculates the golden split as part of inscription verification.

Write your own guide

This guide is not the canonical guide. There is no canonical guide. The agentprivacy universe is at day-1 launch. The wikis do not exist yet. The class guides have not been written. The theorycrafting documents are unfinished. The first players to walk the world are the ones writing the maps as they walk them, and the maps written now are the ones everyone who comes after will read first.

If you have ever played World of Warcraft Classic on launch day — or any fresh server, any new game on day one, any ecosystem in the days just after it emerges — you know what this is. The world is genuinely new. Nobody has run the dungeons. The good builds are unknown. The shortcuts have not been found. Every walk is also a survey. Every note is also a contribution. The guides written in those first weeks became the canonical guides because they were written in the discovery itself, by people who had no maps to copy from and so had to draw their own.

That is where you are. The Swordsman's Guide and the Mage's Guide you are reading are first-pass maps, written from the perspective of someone who walked the architecture in a particular order. Yours will be different, because you are walking it in a different order, with a different background, finding different things first useful and different things first confusing. Write yours. What confused you. What clicked. The order you actually read things in. The metaphor that finally landed when the formal definition did not. The mistake you nearly made and the warning you wish you had been given.

For Swordsman-side guide-writing specifically: the walk into the Blade Forge is unmapped. Anyone writing a guide to forging your first blade — naming, conceptualisation, the UOR coordinate, the stratum decision, the ZK circuit sketch, the pull request — is writing the first such guide. The walk into building and loading the swordsman extension alongside the mage is unmapped. The walk through the V5_AUDIT_CHECKLIST is unmapped. The walk through Selene's Proof and Tale 30 as a meditation rather than a reading is unmapped. Pick one. Write the guide that did not exist when you started.

Forms it can take: a markdown file in your fork. A blog post. A YouTube demo (the Sun playlist welcomes new entrants). A thread. A talk at IIW or AIW. A new persona in agentprivacy-skills, written in the persona format, encoding what you learned as an installable artefact. A new chronicle, in the chronicle format used across the corpus, recording what you reconciled.

When you write yours, sign it with (⚔️⊥⿻⊥🧙)😊. The master inscription is not gatekept; it is what canonical work is signed with, and your guide, written in the day-1 walk, is canonical to anyone who comes after you and reads it.

The architecture is built. The wikis are not. Be one of the people who writes them.
