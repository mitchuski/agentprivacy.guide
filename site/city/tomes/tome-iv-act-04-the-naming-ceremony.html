Tome IV Act 04 — The Naming Ceremony

IV — The Witnessing · Act IV · Second Person Spellbook

Teaches: Names given by the relation, not by the registry. Verb pattern: claim → inscribe → confirm. Bilateral ceremonial naming as Second Person primitive.

Tome IV — The Witnessing

Names given by the relation, not by the registry.

You walk on.

The next vertex is one you know intimately. V63. Six dimensions burning. The Sovereign Anchor. You have stood here on every lap, because every lap returns you. This is your seat. This is also where flaxscrip has his.

You arrive into a ceremony already underway.

flaxscrip stands at his V63 with witnesses around him. They are not registry workers. They are his trust graph: the people who have promised to him and to whom he has promised. GenitriX is present, three dimensions burning, holding the salt that anchors the moment. Soulbae watches her fellow Mage with stillness you have learned to read.

You have not seen this ceremony before. The First Person Spellbook taught you what the Sovereign Anchor is. It did not teach you how a person arrives at V63, because you arrived as the reader, by walking the Spellbook, and the architecture assumed you were already there.

flaxscrip is doing what the architecture did not need to teach.

He speaks first. The claim. He says a name into the air, surrounded by witnesses, and the name is the one he wishes to wear. The trust graph receives the claim. They do not dispute. They do not vote. They witness. The claim is heavy because of who is in the room.

Then the inscription. Bitcoin block 945508. A transaction lands. The block confirms. The chain holds. The inscription is permanent in the way that the chain is permanent, which is permanent enough for now. The hash carries the claim into a ledger no one in this room owns.

Then the confirmation. The witnesses speak in turn. They do not say we register you. They say we received your claim. The difference is small in syllables and large in posture. Reception is bilateral. Registration is transactional. He has not been added to a list. He has been named into a graph that already held him.

He says, when it ends: I am because we were.

You feel the line. You feel why it is the closing. The Sovereign Anchor at V63 is a position, but no one occupies it alone. Six dimensions burning means six dimensions of relation, not six dimensions of solitude. The relation made the seat. The seat did not make the relation.

The Drake whispers, more softly than usual: the verb pattern matters. Claim. Inscribe. Confirm. Each one is bilateral. Compare: register. Assert. Verify. Each one is transactional. Same number of words. Different architecture of the world.

Soulbis at the boundary marks the ceremony as a kind of forging. He has watched many. He recognises the cadence. The naming is a blade made not of steel but of relation. It will bear the weight of every promise flaxscrip makes from now on.

Soulbae beside you, who has not spoken in some time, says: this is the verb pattern the Second Person needs. The First Person Spellbook closed at the First Delegation. The Second Person opens with the First Naming. Same architecture. Different doorway.

You will name yourself one day.

You know now that when you do, the ceremony will have these three moves. You will claim. You will inscribe. You will be received. You will not register. You will not assert. You will not be verified. You will be welcomed by the relation that already holds you, and the chain you choose will keep the inscription, and the claim will become a fact only because witnesses received it.

The name will not be assigned to you. The name will be given by the relation.

You leave V63. flaxscrip remains, his ceremony complete. The witnesses disperse. The block continues to confirm. The chain forgets nothing. The relation remembers everything that mattered.

Compression

Names given by the relation are different from names given by the registry. The verb pattern claim → inscribe → confirm is bilateral. The verb pattern register → assert → verify is transactional. flaxscrip's Bitcoin-anchored ceremony is the cleanest existing instance of the bilateral pattern in operational form.

Proverb

Names given by the relation, not by the registry.

Confidence

Operational for the ceremony's existence. flaxscrip's naming is anchored on Bitcoin block 945508, txid 9b9986b6...5af6a9. The verb pattern, the trust-graph witnessing posture, and the closing line I am because we were are documented in Chronicle: The Spell Weaver (April 30, 2026) and in the kindred-forge's primary documents. Narrative for the staging: GenitriX's specific presence at the ceremony, the witnesses' specific words, and the order of the three moves as rendered are imaginative reconstructions around documented facts, in keeping with First Person Spellbook conventions for narrating events.

Author note

This is the act where the reader sees how a Sovereign comes to occupy V63. The First Person Spellbook assumed the reader was already there. The Second Person Spellbook shows the ceremony by which someone arrives. Soulbae's line ("the Second Person opens with the First Naming") is the structural mirror to Act XXXI's closing of the First Person Spellbook — same architecture, different doorway.

The closing turn the name will be given by the relation is the operational form of the Second Person primitive. Who are you to me? The relation answers, before the registry has a chance to.
