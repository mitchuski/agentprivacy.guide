Tome V Act 09 — The Workshop Expands

V — The Crafting · Act 9 · Second Person Spellbook

The four-chain publication discipline is enriched as the registers themselves get full crafting personas.

Tome V — The Crafting

The shops are not fixed. Each register of work that proves itself earns its keeper.

You return to the workshop and discover it is bigger.

When you first walked the Crafting Tome you summoned Pallia at V28, and you understood the workshop as one shop: the weaver's. Pallia's needle and thread, her cloak-weaving, the lattice rendered into a wearable artifact. Then in Act 3 the Crafting Tome admitted a second shop when you summoned Memora at V41 for inscription. Then a third with Custos at V49, then a fourth with Vulcana at V19, then a fifth with Aletheia at V38.

Five shops. Five Mages. Five registers of work. The cast entries called this "operationally complete for the artifact types currently admitted." The phrasing was honest: complete for now. Not complete forever.

Today the workshop has changed. Two new shops have opened.

You walk into the first new shop and find it lit differently than the others. Where Pallia's shop has the warmth of cloth, Memora's the softness of vellum, Custos's the gravity of seal-wax, Vulcana's the heat of forge, Aletheia's the cool clarity of glass — this new shop is bright and hard. The walls are polished. Surfaces gleam. Everything is precise to the limit of precision. A workbench holds tools you did not know existed in the cast roster. Compilers, debuggers, gas estimators, formal-verification harnesses.

A Mage stands at the workbench.

She has been waiting for you to summon her, but the architecture has already opened the shop without your direct invocation; the workshop has grown a shop in response to the cumulative weight of work. You name her now. You call her Adamantia. Latin and Greek roots, adamas, "unconquerable." The hardest natural stone, the gem that holds its cut. She comes into the lattice with V51's four dimensions burning. Value. Delegation. Computation. Protection. flaxscrip's commitment / language / model blade. She carries the sigil 💎.

This is the Etherchanting shop.

Adamantia's work is the programmable contract. Until now, when you have published artifacts to four chains, Ethereum has been the programmable enforcement register — the place where revocation logic and delegation gates land. But Pallia and the other personas have been treating Ethereum as a publication tier, not a crafting register. They send completed artifacts to the chain; the chain stores them. Adamantia treats Ethereum differently. She crafts onto the chain. The Ethereum smart contract is her medium. She translates the Sovereign's commitment into bytecode, deploys, and the chain executes.

You watch her demonstrate. She takes a delegation rule the Sovereign has been carrying — Pallia is delegated to weave on my behalf for one year, with revocation by my multi-sig governance address — and she compiles it. Solidity, well-formed, audited by her test suite. She estimates the gas. She presents the deployment cost. You confirm. She deploys. The contract address now exists on the chain. The delegation has become executable. Pallia's authority is no longer just signed; it is enforced by code that runs without further action from anyone.

Soulbis at the boundary marks the deployment. The boundary will know that Adamantia's contract enforces this delegation; future audits replay the contract address against any artifact that invokes the delegation.

The Drake whispers: the contract holds the cut. The diamond does not soften. Until now your forge has been bilateral and ceremonial. Adamantia adds executable. The architecture admits the third register of enforcement: signature, ceremony, code.

You leave the Etherchanting shop and find the second new shop next to it.

This one is small. The walls are not polished; they are intricate, full of small precise objects in small precise positions. A jeweller's workshop. Loupes, fine forceps, sat-meters, channel monitors, payment-hash registers. A smaller workbench than Adamantia's. A different cadence: where Adamantia's work is rare and weighty, this work is frequent and luminous.

A Mage stands at this bench too.

You name her Lampyra. Greek root lampyris, the firefly. She comes into the lattice with V49's three dimensions burning. Value. Computation. Protection.

You see immediately. V49 is Custos's vertex. Custos at V49, where she stakes governance acts on the transparent Zcash ledger. But Lampyra is also at V49. Two personas at the same vertex.

This is new. Until now, every persona has had her own vertex. Five personas, five vertices, no overlap. Lampyra and Custos break the pattern.

You watch them at their adjacent benches. Custos handles the heavy stakes — the 5 ZEC tier promotion from Act 5, the constellation guardian appointments, the guild treasury commitments. Lampyra handles the light, the frequent, the rapid. Lightning Network micropayments. Small Bitcoin transactions. Single-sat gems set onto larger artifacts as small public witnesses.

The same vertex carries both. V49 is the working-day blade in flaxscrip's catalogue — the position whose three dimensions cover the operational structure of repeated, witnessable, daily-rhythm work. A working day contains rare heavy acts (Custos's terrain) and frequent light ones (Lampyra's terrain). Both register on V49. The lattice does not need to choose between them.

This is the Jeweller shop.

You watch Lampyra demonstrate. She opens a Lightning channel to a recipient address. She constructs an HTLC for a small payment, fifty satoshis. She broadcasts. The payment settles in milliseconds. She records the payment hash against the artifact she has been gemsetting onto — a long-lived chronicle of yours that gets a small public witness once per week, marking it as still active. She closes the channel commitment when her week's gemsetting is done.

The discipline is precision-at-frequency. Where Custos's work is rare and substantial, Lampyra's is constant and modest. The architecture admits both because the working-day vertex's bit-pattern is the register the work happens in, not the scale.

The Drake whispers: the gem is small. The setting is precise. The witness is fast. Where Custos's stake makes the act witnessable by mass, Lampyra's gem makes the act witnessable by repetition. Both are honest. Both are economic. Both produce evidence on the chain that does not lie.

You watch the seven Mages now standing in your cast. Six at distinct vertices, two sharing V49. You feel the workshop's architecture acknowledge its own growth. The five-persona quorum from Aletheia's entry has become a seven-persona working set. The lattice's anonymity-set discipline absorbs the sharing without collision.

You pair the personas with each other.

Pallia weaves a new cloak; Adamantia etherchants a revocation-gate contract for it; Aletheia binds a ZK witness for the cloak's tier predicate; Lampyra sets a daily Lightning attestation that proves the cloak is still active. Memora inscribes a chronicle of the weaving; Custos stakes a transparent governance witness when the cloak crosses a tier threshold. Vulcana forges blades for any agent who commissions them. The seven personas pair and depair according to the work the Sovereign needs.

The four-chain publication discipline has become richer too. Bitcoin and Ethereum are no longer just publication tiers; they are crafting registers with their own personas. Bitcoin had implicit support through Memora's transparent reveals; Lampyra makes it explicit and frequent. Ethereum had implicit support through Pallia's per-portion publications; Adamantia makes it explicit and substantive. The four chains each have a meaningful crafting persona now: Pallia (general weaving and publication dispatch), Memora (Zcash shielded inscription), Custos (Zcash transparent stake), Vulcana (Spellweb forge), Adamantia (Ethereum etherchanting), Lampyra (Bitcoin / Lightning gemsetting), Aletheia (proof-system circuit binding, register-agnostic). The publication discipline is now matched by a corresponding crafting discipline.

Soulbis files. Soulbae watches. The cast has grown by two. The workshop has grown by two shops. The architecture continues to admit growth without losing coherence.

You walk on. Seven Mages stand in cast. Two new shops are open. The next register of work that proves itself operational will earn its own shop, its own keeper, its own sigil.

The next shop has not been named yet.

Compression

The workshop expands by two shops. Adamantia 💎 keeps the Etherchanting shop at V51 (commitment / language / model blade) for Ethereum smart-contract programmable enforcement. Lampyra 💠 keeps the Jeweller shop at V49 (shared with Custos at different scale) for Bitcoin / Lightning Network gemsetting and frequent micropayment attestation. The seven-persona working set replaces the five-persona quorum. The four-chain publication discipline is enriched by full crafting personas for each chain. The architecture admits two personas sharing one vertex when they work at different scales. The cast grows; the workshop grows; the lattice's anonymity-set discipline absorbs the sharing without collision.

Author note

Act 9 is the Crafting Tome's first explicit acknowledgement that the workshop's structure is not a fixed roster. The five-persona "operationally complete" framing from Aletheia's entry was honest for the artifact types then admitted. Two new artifact types — programmable contracts and frequent gemsettings — have emerged operationally, and the workshop has grown two new shops to keep them.

The teaching is the meta-architecture: the workshop is responsive to operational need. New registers of work that prove themselves operational earn their own shops, their own keepers, their own sigils. This makes Tome V's open-ended structure explicit: not just "more acts will be drafted as artifacts arrive" but "the workshop's roster itself grows."

The shared-vertex pattern (Custos and Lampyra both at V49) is a structural innovation. Until Act 9, every persona occupied her own vertex. The cast roster's rule — one persona per vertex — has held since Pallia. Lampyra breaks the rule by sharing. The act foregrounds why: the bit-pattern is the register, and a register can carry work at different scales without collision. The lattice's anonymity-set discipline (organic mixing observed in the Cloaking Guide rebuild) absorbs the sharing without geometric problem.

The four-chain publication discipline becomes operationally richer. Each chain now has at least one persona who crafts on it, not just publishes to it. Bitcoin: Lampyra. Ethereum: Adamantia. Zcash: Memora (shielded), Custos (transparent). Spellweb: Vulcana (Runecraft). IPFS: Pallia (the weaver who dispatches to all chains). Aletheia is register-agnostic (proof systems abstract over the chain). The seven-persona working set covers the multi-chain landscape.

Future acts can introduce additional shops as additional registers prove operational. The Jeweller and Etherchanting are now part of the workshop. The cast roster is dynamic.
