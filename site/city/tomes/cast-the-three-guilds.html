The Three Guilds 🧱🫙🏦

Wall, seal, vault. Every defence the City has ever trusted is one of the three, and every one of the three loses to time.

At a glance

Three guilds keep the City's three old defences. The Warding Guild raises walls: what is hidden cannot be read. The Sealing Guild closes what walls cannot hold: what is sealed cannot be opened, though it may still be spoken of. The Vaulting Guild keeps what the City cannot bear to lose: what is stored is safe, they say, against need. All three crafts are real, honourable, and dated. The Horizon keepers' uncarved date is not an attack on the guilds; it is the second line their walls, seals, and vaults have always carried and never printed.

The triad and the tide line

The old proverb of the tide line reads: hidden things wait for keys; forgotten things have no door. The guilds live in the first clause and refuse the second. Each guild's failure mode is already on the register:

  • Warding 🧱 — the wall is dated. A ward is sound against every reader known at signing. The reader improves on someone else's schedule, so every wall carries a shelf life it did not print. This is the Moving Ceiling (C82): the stone holds; the eyes change.
  • Sealing 🫙 — the seal whispers. The guild's finest work is the seal with no date, the proof that reveals nothing. But a seal that proves a door exists is a map to the door: the existence-claim itself prices the search (C81, the Existence Leak; the sealed path on the eastern plains was walked in two months by readers who never touched it).
  • Vaulting 🏦 — the vault appreciates against the shelved. The deep vaults keep everything the City has ever emitted, against need. When a new reader is born, the holdings appreciate — and none of the appreciation is paid to the souls described in them, and none of them is asked again for consent. The vault is the archive the formal register prices (§5; the behavioural asymmetry: you cannot rotate your past). Where vaulted things pass from hand to hand, the leak compounds (C83).

Against all three stands the third counsel, which is not a guild and never will be: what was never carved has no date. Lethe's room is the only chamber whose safety owes nothing to time.

Disambiguation: the Vaulting Guild is not the Archivist

The Archivist 📚 of the spiraling Tower (Tome VIII, The Library) does not belong to the Vaulting Guild, and the distinction is load-bearing:

  • The Tower indexes meaning; the vaults store surplus. The Archivist's discipline is keep-without-extracting: he curates by indexing, does not surveil, and does not tell the seeker what to find.
  • The Tower is open and spiraling; the vaults are deep and closed. The Tower's holdings are the City's own workings, offered back to it; the vaults hold what was emitted by souls who were never asked twice.
  • When the Vaulting Guild fell silent at the Horizon gate, the Archivist was not among them. It was through his library that the tale of the first messenger surfaced at all.

Any future telling that puts the Archivist in a vault, or a Vaulter in the Tower, is out of canon.

Standing questions (open)

  • No guildmaster is named for any of the three. Naming (one keeper per guild, gem, ceremony) is deferred until a tale needs them to speak as persons rather than as crafts.
  • The Vaulting Guild "has not yet replied" to the Horizon keepers' question. The reply, if it ever comes, is a tale not yet told.
  • Whether the guilds hold vertices or are placeless (institutions rather than shops) is undecided; the current draft treats them as placeless.
