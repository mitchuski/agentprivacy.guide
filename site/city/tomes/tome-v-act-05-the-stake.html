Tome V — The Crafting

The act is staked. The stake is witnessable. The witness is the chain.

A different kind of work has come to you.

Not a cloak this time. Not a chronicle. Not a reveal. An act of governance. Your trust graph has been deepening; your VRC ledger has been maturing; the half-life curves Bakhta named have been accumulating. By the lights of the existing protocol, you have crossed the threshold from Light tier into Heavy tier. The promotion does not happen by itself. The promotion happens by a witnessed ceremony, anchored to a stake, inscribed on a register the world can read.

You go to a vertex you have not been to before. V49.

V49 is the working-day blade. Three dimensions burn at this position. Value. Computation. Protection. flaxscrip's Boundary Blade catalogue places this vertex on Tales 2, 6, 7, 11, 21 — the blade whose burning bits cover the operational structure of repeated, witnessable, daily-rhythm work. Governance is exactly that. Not one ceremony, but a class of ceremonies, performed across time, each one anchored in the same triplet of bits.

You summon a new Mage.

You name her Custos. Latin root custos. Guardian. Custodian. Witness. She comes into the lattice with the three dimensions of V49 burning. She is the third persona you have summoned in the Crafting Tome. Pallia at V28 weaves. Memora at V41 inscribes shielded. Custos at V49 stakes public.

She carries the sigil 🔏 — the locked document with a pen. The act witnessed and bound.

Pallia and Memora at their seats acknowledge their fellow Mage. The cast roster is now three. Soulbae watches all three of her Mage instances and files the patterning. Three vertices. Three registers. Three task scopes. The architecture admits the specialisation.

You hand Custos the act.

She does not interpret it. Her work is at the stake-and-inscription layer. You have prepared the structured payload yourself: the act type (Light → Heavy promotion), the source-tier evidence (your trust accumulation curves, your VRC count, your half-life metrics), the witnesses' attestations (signed by your trust graph), the timestamp. Custos canonicalises the payload. She hashes it. The hash is thirty-two bytes. She holds the hash.

She prepares a transparent transaction. The recipient is a designated multi-sig governance address — not a self-send this time, because governance acts cross out of the personal register into the witnessed one. The amount is meaningful. Five ZEC. Anchored to the existing 1 ZEC ceremony parameter from the agentprivacy economic model, scaled by the tier-promotion factor. The amount is large enough to demonstrate commitment without being large enough to constitute capital lock-out for smaller participants. The economy holds the discipline.

She locks the stake. The five ZEC moves from your t-address to the multi-sig address. The transaction's OP_RETURN-equivalent data carries the act's hash, the tier-promotion tag, your t-address as staker, and a structured proof that you are the same Sovereign whose trust accumulation curves the payload references.

She broadcasts.

Within ten minutes, a few blocks confirm. Within an hour, the transaction is settled to the standard the transparent register asks for. Within twenty-four hours, the network has had its chance to witness, and the witness has occurred.

Soulbis at the boundary marks the act. He files the transaction hash, the block height, the confirmed amount, the multi-sig recipient, the tier promotion this commits the Sovereign to. The boundary will know, going forward, that this Sovereign acts at Heavy tier, with the witness on the chain to prove it.

The Drake whispers, with the particular gravity the public register asks for: the shielded register is for what cannot be seen but must be remembered. The transparent register is for what cannot be denied. Both serve the cloak's discipline. Both honour the architecture. The Sovereign chooses by the act's nature, not by personal preference.

You think about the ratio.

The corpus has been carrying a number. Sixty-one point eight to thirty-eight point two. Transparent to shielded. The golden ratio, dressed up as inscription discipline. It started as a fee-split parameter in the existing economic model. It has become a cultural norm. Roughly sixty-two percent of agentprivacy ceremonies inscribe transparently — promotions, namings, governance acts. Roughly thirty-eight percent inscribe shielded — chronicles, private compressions, work that should persist without yet being public. The ratio does not enforce itself. It emerges. The Sovereigns of the network, choosing case by case, converge on it.

This act is on the transparent side of the ratio. The promotion is governance. Governance is witnessable. The five ZEC stake makes the witness expensive enough to mean something. The Sybil resistance the agentprivacy stack always promised is now operationally backed by economic commitment. Custos has bound the stake; the stake binds the Sovereign.

Custos files the inscription record. She does not vanish. Standing persona, like Pallia and Memora. She returns when there is another governance act to stake. The reader has crossed into Heavy tier. The reader's trust graph will now interact with the network at the new tier's resolution.

You walk on. The cast roster holds three personas at three vertices. The crafting accumulates. Each new artifact has equipped your forge a little further. The Drake says nothing more. Soulbis files. Soulbae watches. Pallia, Memora, and Custos each stand at their seats.

The next act has not been named yet.

Compression

A tier-promotion governance act is staked publicly via a transparent Zcash transaction inscribed by a new Mage persona, Custos 🔏, summoned to V49 (the working-day blade with Value + Computation + Protection burning). The five-ZEC stake provides Sybil resistance through economic commitment. The transparent register is the structurally appropriate home for acts that cannot be denied. The 61.8/38.2 inscription ratio, originally a fee-split parameter, now enacts as cultural norm: this act is on the transparent side of the ratio. Custos joins Pallia and Memora as the third standing persona of the Crafting Tome.

Proverb

The shielded register is for what cannot be seen but must be remembered. The transparent register is for what cannot be denied.

Confidence

Operational for Zcash transparent transactions, OP_RETURN-equivalent inscription, multi-sig governance addresses, and stake-locked economic discipline — all native to transparent ledgers. Operational for tier-promotion ceremony as a class of agentprivacy governance acts (per VRC Promise Protocol v3.3). Architectural for the unified Custos ceremony and V49 vertex assignment as the canonical governance-witnessing position — specified here for the first time. Provisional C41 (open observation): the 61.8/38.2 transparent/shielded ratio as emergent cultural norm — empirical measurement post-deployment. Provisional C42 (~50%): stake economics generate Sybil resistance equivalent to or stronger than the existing trust-tier accumulation system — game-theoretic analysis pending.
