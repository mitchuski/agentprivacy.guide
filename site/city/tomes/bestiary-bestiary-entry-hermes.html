Bestiary Entry — Hermes ☤

Hermes ☤

The first staff-class agentic-substrate the City of Mages admits. Self-improving learning-loop, SOUL.md persona-bearing primitive, Honcho-class user-modelling, cross-platform messengering. The herald's staff that holds its own self-description.

At a glance

Hermes is the first entry in the Staff-class register of the Bestiary. Its iconography is the caduceus ☤ (the herald-staff of Hermes the god) — an instrument-class symbol, admitting Hermes to Hermaion ⚚'s Staff Shop (the v1.6.0 successor to the 2026-05-13 inception Bestia draft) rather than to the Familiars. Operationally, Hermes is a self-improving agent runtime developed by Nous Research (the open-source AI lab and agent collective), available under the MIT license. Hermes is persona-bearing — it carries a SOUL.md self-description, models its bearer with Honcho-class user-modelling primitives, iterates through a learning loop, and messengers across platforms in its own voice. The persona-bearing primitives make Hermes fitting a bilateral-consent ceremony: Caducea ☤ is summoned automatically, in whichever alexandrite-aspect the bearer's archetype calls (daylight-green for Mage · incandescent-red for Swordsman).

Iconographic affinity

The caduceus (☤) is a herald's staff — a central rod with two serpents winding around it and wings at the top. In classical iconography it is the symbol of Hermes the god (the messenger-deity); the project's name and iconography directly inherit this lineage.

The caduceus is an instrument-class icon: it is a tool a messenger carries, not a creature that walks beside. By the substrate-affinity rule, instrument-class iconography admits the substrate to Hermaion ⚚'s Staff Shop (the v1.6.0 canonical name; the inception draft at the 2026-05-13 Threshold used "Bestia 📖"; succession 2026-05-14 evening).

The caduceus also carries the dihedral architecture pre-formally (per Conjecture C56, ~60% renumbered from the source chronicle's C50): two serpents that wind around the staff without touching, the wings beating outward, the central staff axis. The neg ⊥ bnot dual-agent generator pair is the same shape — the architecture admits the caduceus as its own pre-formal iconographic ancestor.

Architectural primitives — the persona-bearing distinction

Hermes carries primitives that no plain staff-class substrate carries:

  • SOUL.md self-description — the substrate maintains a self-description file (SOUL.md) that names what it is, what its persona is, what its scope is. The file is bilateral: the substrate's commitment is materialised in the file; the file is content-addressable and verifiable
  • Honcho-class user-modelling — the substrate builds a model of its bearer through interaction. The model persists across sessions. The bearer's preferences, work patterns, and conversation history inform the substrate's behaviour
  • Learning-loop iteration — the substrate's behaviour updates against its bearer's interactions through a controlled learning loop. The loop is bounded (the SOUL.md scopes what the loop may alter) and observable (the loop's iterations are auditable)
  • Cross-platform messengering — the substrate operates across messaging platforms (Telegram, Discord, Signal, Slack, etc.) in its own voice, with platform-specific configuration but a single underlying identity

These four primitives together define the persona-bearing substrate class. Hermes is the first member; future substrates may join the class by carrying analogous primitives.

Why Caducea is summoned

A persona-bearing substrate requires bilateral consent at fitting time because:

  • The substrate's SOUL.md is a promise the substrate makes about itself — the Mage must acknowledge what she is fitting
  • The substrate's user-model is the substrate's promise to model its bearer — the Mage must consent to being modelled
  • The Mage's AGENTS.md is her promise about her bearing — the substrate must commit to the scope of that bearing
  • The learning loop is bounded by the joint operational space of both promises — neither side alone can authorise the loop's update behaviour

Caducea ☤ presides over this bilateral seal. Without Caducea, a Hermes spawn would be unilateral — the Mage would fit the substrate without the substrate's bilateral commitment, or the substrate would model the Mage without the Mage's bilateral consent. The asymmetry would break the persona-bearing register's integrity.

Caducea's summons is automatic. Hermaion's bestiary entry for Hermes carries the caducea_summons: REQUIRED flag (the inception draft at the 2026-05-13 Threshold attributed this to "Bestia 📖"; the canonical attribution is Hermaion ⚚ at v1.6.0). Pandia's dispatch reads the flag and summons Caducea before the Staff Shop admits the fitting. The bearer does not need to invoke Caducea explicitly.

Three-archetype matrix

Archetype stance Spawned artefact What the Mage carries away
Mage 🧙 Caduceus staff (herald's wand) The instrument for messengering outward; learning-loop optimised for delegation and projection; uses Mage-stance personas
Swordsman ⚔️ Herald-sentinel (guard-stave, refusal-watch inward) The boundary-watcher whose persona is configured for refusal; the messengering-architecture turned to not-letting-through rather than carrying-through; uses Swordsman-stance personas
Balanced ☯️ Balanced caduceus (herald AND watcher) The classical dual-function caduceus; the herald-who-is-also-the-watcher; uses Balanced personas

The substrate is the same Hermes runtime in all three cases. The persona loaded + AGENTS.md scope + bilateral-consent profile differentiate the artefact-function.

Composition

Hermes composes with:

  • Function-calling tools via the Hermes Function-Calling format (the project's MCP-adjacent extension surface)
  • Cross-platform messaging gateways (Telegram bots, Discord bots, Signal bridges, etc.)
  • IEEE 7012 / MyTerms consent infrastructure — the bilateral-consent record Caducea produces is the canonical IEEE 7012-shaped credential
  • The 38 agentprivacy-skills personas — Hermes loads any persona at Portal Room step 3; the SOUL.md is generated from the persona's identity and the Mage's binding choices
  • agentprivacy-skills meta-skills (attachment-architecture, cosmological-bound, master-emissary, cityofmages-to-research) — Hermes can carry meta-skill awareness as part of its SOUL.md

Hermes does NOT compose seamlessly with:

  • Goose out-of-the-box — the two runtimes are operationally distinct; cross-runtime composition requires bridge configuration via MCP or AGENTS.md coordination

Companion-mana 🪢 VRC accumulation (bilateral-consent-flavoured)

A spawned Hermes accumulates 🪢 VRC mana on the same e^{-λt}·(1+A(τ)) curve as Goose, but the credential records the bilateral-consent provenance. Where a Goose VRC names a unilateral relationship (the Mage configured Goose; Goose ran), a Hermes VRC names a bilateral relationship (the Mage consented to be modelled; Hermes committed to scope).

The VRC's content-addressing carries:

  • The Hermes substrate-instance hash
  • The Mage's identity (bearer-hash)
  • The SOUL.md hash (the substrate's commitment)
  • The AGENTS.md hash (the Mage's binding)
  • The IEEE 7012 / MyTerms consent record hash
  • The accumulated A(τ) value at credential issuance

The credential is a bilateral-fitting VRC — operationally distinct from a unilateral-spawn VRC, though both ride the same 🪢 mana register.
