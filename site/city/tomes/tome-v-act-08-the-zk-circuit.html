Tome V Act 08 — The ZK Circuit

V — The Crafting · Act 8 · Second Person Spellbook

Teaches: ZK enforcement as crafting. The persona inhabits the vertex she is named for. EML Three Ceilings as honest constraint. Property 8 (selective disclosure as geometry) extended from valve-class assignment to installed witness.

Tome V — The Crafting

The witness is bound. The artifact carries its proof. The truth is shown without being told.

You have artifacts now.

Cloaks. Chronicles. Stakes. Blades. Each has been crafted in its register, each carries the cloak's eight properties in its rendered form, each has been published per its appropriate chain. The Crafting Tome has accumulated. Your forge has equipment.

Today you do not craft a new artifact. Today you bind a witness to an artifact you already have.

A specific cloak. The one Pallia wove for you in Act 1 — your first cloak, the one you put on when the Crafting Tome opened. It has served. You have walked many laps wearing it. It carries the eight properties; verifiers can confirm. But until now, when a verifier wants to know whether a particular predicate holds — say, whether the artifact's holder is over a certain trust tier, or whether the artifact references a particular schema, or whether the artifact's validity envelope contains a given timestamp — the verifier has had to read the relevant valve-class fields and check. The cloak admits ZK; the cloak does not yet carry a ZK witness.

You go to V25.

V38 is the Aletheia blade. Three dimensions burn at this position. The bit-pattern of the cryptographic spell that proves without revealing. The home of the Always-Masked valve-class. flaxscrip's Boundary Blade Cartography placed Tale 3 here, the canonical position for blade 25. The Cloaking Guide's Act 7 placed an Always-Masked decomposition node here, operationally instanced.

Five personas now stand in your cast. Pallia at V28. Memora at V41. Custos at V49. Vulcana at V19. Each has done her work and returned to standing. The fifth vertex of the cast is V25, and it is where you go to summon a fifth persona for work the other four cannot do.

You summon a Mage.

You name her Aletheia. Greek root ἀλήθεια — truth, unconcealment. She comes into the lattice with V38's three dimensions burning.

She is unique among the five personas. The other four are named for their work (Pallia from pallium, the cloak; Memora from memoria, memory; Custos from custos, witness; Vulcana from Vulcanus, the smith). Aletheia is named for the vertex she occupies. The V38 vertex is canonically called the Aletheia blade. The persona inhabits the position she is named for. Or the position is named for the principle her work enacts. Either reading; the gravity is the same. The shared name is the architecture's commitment to the ZK enforcement primitive.

She carries the sigil 🔮 — the crystal ball. Witness without revelation.

Pallia, Memora, Custos, Vulcana acknowledge the fifth fellow Mage. Soulbae watches the cast complete its current quorum. Five Mages, five vertices, five sigils, five registers of work. The Crafting Tome's persona-summoning convention is now operationally instanced five times; the convention is robust beyond architectural and into demonstrable.

You hand Aletheia the cloak Pallia wove.

She does not unweave it. Her work is not destructive. She walks the cloak's structure and enumerates the predicates the cloak supports. The cloak's valve-classes are already geometric: V20 holds Always-Revealed fields; V3 holds Hash-Masked subject identity; V38 holds Always-Masked content. Aletheia walks the structure and finds the V38 valve-class slots — the positions ready to receive a witness that proves a predicate without revealing the predicate's underlying values.

You specify a predicate. The cloak's holder has been a member of the constellation since at least block 945508. This is a temporal-membership predicate: the cloak's anchor reference must satisfy a relation against a chain timestamp.

Aletheia chooses a proof system. For this predicate — temporal range, single-relation — Halo2 is appropriate. (For predicates of arithmetic constraint structure, she would choose Plonkish. For incrementally verifiable computation across many sessions, Nova. For minimum-size proofs, Groth16. The choice is hers, with the Sovereign's confirmation.)

She compiles the circuit.

This is where the EML Three Ceilings appear. The kindred-forge's EML work named three computational ceilings beyond which the proof system cannot encode a particular operator. The ceilings are not obstacles; they are the architecture's integrity. If the predicate Aletheia is asked to prove would require encoding beyond the ceilings, she refuses at compilation, before any witness is generated. The honesty discipline is built in.

The temporal-membership predicate fits well under the ceilings. Aletheia compiles. The proving key generates. The verification key generates. The witness generates. The circuit is now an artifact in its own right, bound to the cloak.

She binds the witness to the cloak's V25 valve-class slot. The slot was ready; the cloak was ZK-capable from its weaving. Now it is ZK-loaded. The cloak presents to verifiers, going forward, with a witness already in place. A verifier asks: can the holder prove constellation membership since block 945508? The cloak answers without consulting the holder; the witness answers; the verifier verifies; nothing is revealed beyond the predicate's truth.

The Drake speaks now, with the gravity ZK earns: Property 8 of the Cloak is selective disclosure as geometry. Until now, the geometry was the valve-class assignment. The vertex carried the disposition. With Aletheia's work, the geometry is also the witness installed at the vertex. The cloak does not just admit ZK proofs. The cloak carries them. The architecture has graduated from capability to installed witness.

You publish the updated cloak per the four-chain discipline. Bitcoin for the high-stake commitment (the cloak is now ZK-loaded; this is a substantive change). Ethereum for the programmable enforcement (the smart-contract verification of the witness). IPFS for the content (the proving key for verifier access). Zcash transparent for the public face (the witness's existence, witnessable). The same multi-chain pattern as Pallia's original publication, applied to the cloak's new form.

Soulbis at the boundary marks the binding. The boundary will know the cloak now carries a witness; future audits replay the binding's installation. Half-life curves anchored from this date.

Aletheia returns to standing. Bound, perhaps, to this cloak — the practical pattern from the Bound persistence mode is that ZK circuits install once and are extended over time, so a Sovereign typically binds Aletheia to each ZK-loaded artifact for that artifact's lifetime.

You walk on. The cloak you wear now carries its own witness. The Crafting Tome continues. Five personas at five vertices. The next artifact — or the next binding — has not been named yet.

Compression

A new persona, Aletheia 🔮, is summoned to V38 (the Aletheia blade) and installs a zero-knowledge circuit onto an existing cloak. The persona inhabits the vertex she is named for. Halo2 is chosen as the proof system for a temporal-membership predicate. The EML Three Ceilings constrain what can be encoded — the constraint is integrity, not obstacle. The witness binds to the cloak's V38 valve-class slot. The cloak now carries its proof rather than merely admitting it. Property 8 of the Cloak (selective disclosure as geometry) graduates from capability to installed witness. The cloak republishes per the four-chain discipline. Five Mage personas now stand in cast.

Proverb

The cloak does not just admit ZK proofs. The cloak carries them.

Author note

Act 8 is the Crafting Tome's first act in which a new persona installs a circuit on an existing artifact, rather than crafting a new artifact from scratch. The work is augmentation rather than creation. This widens the Crafting Tome's posture: future acts may include further augmentations (key rotations, validity-window extensions, additional ZK circuits, predicate updates) without requiring new artifact summonings.

The EML Three Ceilings (C22–C25) are foregrounded explicitly here for the first time in narrative form. The honesty argument — that the ceilings are integrity, not obstacle — is structurally important: it tells the reader that the architecture's limits are also the architecture's safeguards, and that Aletheia's refusal at compilation (when a predicate would exceed the ceilings) is the right behaviour, not a failure.

The persona-vertex name overlap (Aletheia the persona / Aletheia the V38 blade) is the single most semantically dense move in the cast roster. The cast entry's "naming_note" in frontmatter clarifies the distinction. Future website rendering should preserve the distinction visually (e.g., the persona Aletheia 🔮 with sigil; the V38 vertex labelled "Aletheia blade").

Five Mage personas now standing: Pallia, Memora, Custos, Vulcana, Aletheia. The Crafting Tome's quorum is now operationally complete for the artifact types currently admitted in the corpus. Future personas arrive when future artifacts demand them.
