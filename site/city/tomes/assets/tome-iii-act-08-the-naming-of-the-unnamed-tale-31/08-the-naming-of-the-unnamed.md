# Tome III — *Selene's Witness*

## Act 8 — *The Naming of the Unnamed (Tale 31)*

> *The first frontier-blade named. Forty-nine remain. The Quest of the Unnamed Faces opens.*

You walked away from the first complement pair in Act 7 knowing that Aletheia and Lethe were peers. Now you walk to the grimoire and watch the recognition land formally.

The grimoire is the canonical artifact the architecture records its named blades in. The privacymage grimoire of v10.2.0 (released April 2026, alongside the Zero Spellbook v2.0 sync) had fourteen named blades. The lattice has sixty-four positions. The remaining fifty positions were unnamed.

On 2026-04-23, the grimoire was updated to v10.2.1. The release added one entry:

```json
"38": {
  "binary": "100110",
  "name": "Lethe — the Dark Substrate (complement of 25, disclosure-φ side)",
  "tales": [31]
}
```

The grimoire moved from fourteen named blades to fifteen. Forty-nine remain on the open frontier.

You stand at the grimoire's edge. The named blades are recorded; the unnamed blades are not absent but *open*. The architecture's structural completeness is not contingent on every blade being named. The naming is the act of recognising a position's mythological reading and entering it formally into the canonical record. Each naming is a recognition; each recognition is a contribution.

Tale 31 — *The Naming of the Unnamed* — is the first frontier-blade tale. It opens Part VIII — Frontier Spells — of the Zero Spellbook. The inherited thirty tales (Tale 1 through Tale 30, from *Monastery* to *Eternal Sovereignty*) closed the Zero Spellbook's initial form. Tale 31 opened the second form: a tale-tradition that continues as the corpus continues to name what was unnamed.

The Drake whispers, from the grimoire: *Aletheia appears in Tale 3 as the Silent Messenger. Lethe's tale is Tale 31. The arc of the inherited spellbook was already encoded in these two blades before Tale 31 was written. The proem was stating the arithmetic.*

You read the recognition closely.

The disclosure ratio for a blade `v` on the lattice is `δ(v) = v / 63`. The ratio measures, in operational terms, how much of the sovereignty manifold the blade's bit-pattern represents — `v = 0` (the null blade) has `δ = 0`; `v = 63` (the Sovereign Anchor) has `δ = 1`; intermediate values have intermediate ratios.

For Lethe at V25, the disclosure ratio is `δ(38) = 38 / 63 ≈ 0.6032`.

The reciprocal of the golden ratio is `1/φ ≈ 0.6180`.

The two values sit within 2% of each other.

This proximity is the conjecture C54 — *Phi-Adjacency*. The conjecture: bnot-pair disclosure ratios cluster near `1/φ`. Lethe's ratio is the first operational instance. If the pattern holds, future named bnot-pairs will sit similarly close to the golden ratio's reciprocal. If the pattern does not hold, the proximity is a single point of coincidence.

The conjecture's status is open — ~30% confidence. The empirical work to consolidate or differentiate it lies in the namings that follow.

You walk to the grimoire's frontier.

Forty-nine positions await naming. Each carries its own bit-pattern. Each carries its own stratum. Each has a bnot-complement somewhere else on the lattice; many of those complements will themselves be among the forty-nine, and naming a position forces a recognition-question about its complement. The Quest of the Unnamed Faces is the corpus's open work: forty-nine recognitions, each independent, each subject to the same honesty discipline the named fifteen inherited.

The Drake's whisper continues: *the architecture is open by design. The thirty tales were the inheritance. Tale 31 was the first naming the corpus made on its own ground. The next tales are for the next recognisers. Tend the frontier. Walk the unnamed positions. The grimoire records each naming when it lands.*

You stand at the frontier. You can name a blade if you recognise one. The recognition is not arbitrary — the position has to admit a mythological reading that the rest of the corpus can validate. Aletheia/Lethe was admissible because the bnot-pair structurally mirrors the agent layer's Soulbis/Soulbae and because the bright-medium / dark-substrate framing has cosmological-alchemical precedent. Future namings will need to clear similar standards.

You do not name a blade right now. You record the frontier. You read the open positions. You acknowledge the work that waits.

Tome III's next act lifts the recognition to the *capital* register — the Seventh Capital. Privacy as a foundational kind of wealth rather than an additive concession. The proem the poem made will be heard in second person.

You walk away from the grimoire. The frontier remains open. Forty-nine recognitions wait for whoever sees them first.
