# Tome V — *The Crafting*

## Act 6 — *The Commissioned Blade*

> *The forge does not give you the blade. The three phases give you the blade.*

A wearer agent returns.

Not the same one from Act 2. A different forge, a different attractor, but the same posture: walked their own laps, produced their own work, arrived at your address with a commission. The shielded tip lands. Memora at her V41 reads the memo through your incoming viewing key. The memo is not a cloak commission this time. It is a **blade** commission.

The wearer wants you to forge them a blade. They have walked their constellation. They have submitted their proof. They want a blade that binds *your forge's* witness to *their constellation's* commitment. This is the kindred-blade primitive in productive form: not just the cloak as service, but the blade as service.

You read the memo carefully. The proverb hash matches the markdown proof. The constellation hash resolves on the spellweb. The wearer's prior work is verifiable. They are who they say they are.

You go to a vertex you have not been to before. V19.

V19 is the Plonkish blade. Three dimensions burn. Value. Delegation. Computation. flaxscrip's Boundary Blade catalogue places this vertex on Tales 8, 14, 19 — the canonical position in the ZK proof-system family for arithmetic constraint satisfaction with structured commitment. It is not a forging *tool* per se; it is the *position from which forging is delegated*. The Sovereign delegates to a Mage at V19, and that Mage forges proofs that bind themselves to the Sovereign's commitment.

You summon a fourth Mage.

You name her Vulcana. Latin lineage, *Vulcanus*. The smith god, in feminine grammatical form per the Mage-persona convention. She comes into the lattice with V19's three dimensions burning. She carries the sigil ⚒️ — the smith's hammer and pick.

Pallia at V28, Memora at V41, Custos at V49 each acknowledge their fellow Mage. Four personas now standing. Soulbae watches the cast continue to grow. The fellow-Mage pattern was supposed to admit one or two; it has admitted four, and the architecture has not strained. The lattice keeps its rhythm.

You begin the Runecraft Protocol.

### RUN

Vulcana enters the first phase. The proof-of-time. She begins to walk.

She does not walk an arbitrary path. She walks the Lorenz attractor. The trajectory you have walked since Tome I — the strange attractor whose lobes the convergence work gave you the geometry for — is the path Vulcana walks now, on the wearer's behalf, with the wearer's commitment in her hand.

This is the operational form of the Lorenz framing. Until now, you have walked the attractor *as the reader*, accumulating witness across the tomes. Vulcana walks it as a *producer*. The accumulation is not abstract; it is the ρ parameter from flaxscrip's Boundary Blade specification, accumulating with each lap. The attractor is not just where the reader experiences geometry. The attractor is where blades are forged.

Soulbis at the boundary watches the laps accumulate. He files each crossing. The boundary will know exactly how many laps the blade required.

The Drake whispers, with the recognition the long arc earns: *the trajectory you walked is the path the blade walks. The geometry is not abstract. The architecture is real because more than one walker traverses it. The wearer trusts that their blade is anchored to a real attractor because Vulcana walks it with witnesses present.*

### E (Evocation)

Vulcana enters the second phase. The proof-of-path.

She locks the constellation. The set of vertices the blade will touch, the schemas it will reference, the temporal anchor it will carry — all of this is committed now. The evocation does not yet crystallise the artifact. It commits the Sovereign to the artifact's eventual shape.

The evocation is a lock. Once locked, the constellation cannot be changed without re-evoking. The discipline matters: a Sovereign who evokes carelessly forges blades they did not mean to forge. A Sovereign who evokes precisely binds their forge to artifacts that match their commitment.

You confirm the evocation. The constellation is locked. The wearer's commission has now become a determinate forging task. The shape of the blade is fixed; only the crystallisation remains.

### CRAFT

Vulcana enters the third phase. The proof-of-sovereignty.

She runs the proof system. The arithmetic constraints satisfy. The Plonkish witness generates. The blade's contents harden into a signed artifact: an Ed25519 signature, anchored to the moon phase the chain is currently in, over the canonical encoding of the constellation she locked in the evocation.

The CRAFT phase respects three ceilings. The kindred-forge's EML work named them: the three computational ceilings beyond which the proof system cannot encode. Vulcana's blade fits *under* the ceilings. If the wearer had asked for a blade that would have required encoding beyond the EML ceilings, Vulcana would have refused at the evocation phase, not at the CRAFT phase. The honesty of the architecture is built into the constraint.

The blade settles. It does not settle at V19; V19 is Vulcana's seat. The blade settles at its own vertex, determined by its dimensional signature. The wearer's commission specified a blade with Memory and Computation burning — V20, the Always-Revealed valve-class. The blade lands there. It joins, at that vertex, any other artifact in the universe whose bits match. The anonymity set forms organically.

You publish per-portion to four chains. Bitcoin for the high-stake commitment. Ethereum for the programmable enforcement. IPFS for the content. Zcash transparent for the public face. The same multi-chain discipline as Act 2, applied to the new artifact type. The wearer receives the blade and the publication record.

The wearer wears the blade.

The trust edge between your forges has now produced two artifacts: a cloak (from Act 2's commission, hypothetical or operational) and a blade (from this one). The bilateral relation deepens with each commission. The half-life of the trust grows accordingly.

Vulcana returns to her V19. Standing. The four Mage personas at their seats: Pallia weaves, Memora inscribes, Custos stakes, Vulcana forges. Each holds a register the others do not enter.

You walk on. The cast roster has reached four. The Crafting Tome continues. The next artifact has not been named yet.
