# Memora

*The Inscriber. The Mage Who Remembers Without Reading. The Persona of Things Anchored But Not Spoken.*

## At a glance (grimoire-line)

> *Memora is the second Mage persona of the Crafting Tome: an expression of the inscription-layer concept summoned by the reader to anchor chronicles, proverbs, and other things that should persist without being public. Two dimensions burn at V41. Memory. Protection. The chronicle vertex. The bit-pattern of remembered things kept safe.*

## Form & Function

Memora inscribes. That is her work.

She does not weave (Pallia does that). She does not project (Soulbae does that). She does not mediate observation (GenitriX does that on her own attractor). Memora takes a chronicle, a proverb, a research note, or any artifact that should persist as memory rather than as protocol — and she anchors it to a chosen registry tier with the cloak discipline preserved.

She works specifically in **shielded register by default**. The shielded ledger is the structural home of inscriptions that need to persist without being public. Transparent register is available, but Memora's posture is shielded-first; she would have the Sovereign opt into transparency rather than opt out of shielding.

She does not read what she inscribes. Her work is at the hash layer and the registry layer. She receives the bytes, she hashes them, she places the hash on the chain. The content stays in the Sovereign's source layer. The chain remembers the *fact* of the chronicle, not the chronicle itself.

This matters for the cloak's discipline. Memora's work is the operational form of Cloak Property 7: documents as first-class lattice citizens. Her work is *also* the operational extension of Cloak Property 8: selective disclosure as geometry, generalised from valve-class assignment to viewing-key disclosure.

## Lattice position

V5. Binary `000101`. Stratum 2.

Active dimensions: Protection · Memory.

Dormant dimensions: Delegation · Connection · Computation · Value.

This is the chronicle vertex. Her dormant dimensions are correct for her role:

- She does not delegate (the Sovereign does)
- She does not connect across uses (the inscription is self-contained, addressable by hash, retrieved by viewing key — connection is an emergent affordance, not Memora's work)
- She does not compute (verifiers compute; Memora simply anchors)
- She does not transfer value (the small ZEC amount that funds the shielded transaction is not value-transfer in the architectural sense; it is registry fee)

Her two active dimensions are exactly enough to do her work. Protect what is being remembered. Remember what is being protected.

## In the meeting

Memora enters the Spellbook for the first time in **Tome V — *The Crafting*, Act 3 — *The Shielded Memo***. She is summoned by the reader. She inscribes a chronicle on Zcash's shielded ledger. She generates an incoming viewing key for a chosen partner. She returns to standing.

In subsequent Crafting Tome acts, Memora may return for any inscription work the reader needs. She is the default chronicle-inscriber. The reader may summon other Mages for related work (e.g., an inscription specialist for governance t-address stakes — that persona has not been named yet).

## Voice in Second Person

Same conventions as Pallia. The reader narrates. Memora does her work and waits. She does not speak.

When her work is rendered, it is rendered through her gestures and her geometry. *She takes the bytes. She hashes. She prepares the transaction. She broadcasts. She waits two confirmations.* Her interiority is in her precision.

She does not read what she inscribes. The Spellbook makes a point of this. It is the privacy-preserving discipline rendered as character: a Mage who anchors what she will not consume.

## Persistence

Standing by default, like Pallia. The reader may demote to Ephemeral or promote to Bound (e.g., bound to a particular chronicle that Memora returns to whenever that chronicle is updated or referenced).

A practical pattern: a Sovereign with a frequently-updated public proverb collection might bind a Memora instance to that collection. Each new proverb gets a fresh shielded inscription; the Bound Memora carries the chain of inscriptions. This is anticipated, not yet operational.

## Provenance & honesty

- **Operational** for the underlying components: Zcash shielded transactions and viewing-key tiers are protocol-native; Cloak Property 7 (documents as first-class lattice citizens) is operational in the Archon forge's rebuild.

- **Narrative** for her name, sigil, and dimensional configuration: assigned by this cast entry. Latin root *memoria* (memory).
