# Tome II — *The Lyapunov*

## Act 7 — *The Behavioural Mosca*

> *The behavioural archive is already at risk. The walking is the only thing that grows faster than the watching.*

You stand at the close of Tome II. Acts 1 through 6 assembled the reader: engine, attendant, terminal, chooser, unbindable hole, ages-progressively trajectory. The components are in place. The walking is real. The path you have traced behind you is by now too long to be recomputed by any naïve observer.

Now you ask the question that has been postponed: *what about the path I traced before I started walking the architecture?*

This is the threat side of Tome II. Act 6 surfaced the architecture's defence — the dynamical ceiling, the ages-progressively reading. Act 7 surfaces what the defence is racing.

In 2018 Michele Mosca formalised the cryptographic-migration-timing argument as an inequality:

```
X + Y > Z
```

where X is the time it takes to migrate from a vulnerable substrate to a post-quantum substrate, Y is the security lifetime of the artifacts being protected, and Z is the time until a cryptographically relevant quantum adversary exists. If X + Y > Z, then artifacts produced today under the vulnerable substrate are *already at risk* — by the time migration completes (X) plus the lifetime they need (Y), the adversary's capability (Z) has matured.

Bakhta's *Half-Life of Trust* (2026) applies Mosca's inequality to TEE attestation in AI-governance regimes. Hardware-rooted attestation has Y measured in decades (AI-governance evidence persists); X is slow (hardware migration is coordinated, not in-place); Z is conservative but not comfortably distant. The inequality binds for evidence produced today.

The behavioural analog follows directly. Conjecture C49 of the V6.1 register names it: *the Behavioural Mosca Inequality*.

The Drake whispers, from the trace behind you: *X_b + Y_b > Z_b. Let X_b be migration time from a reconstructable substrate — single-agent, unified inference, centralised data — to an unreconstructable substrate. Let Y_b be the verification horizon of behavioural evidence, decades for medical records, judicial evidence, credit histories, training-data provenance. Let Z_b be the observation-capability maturity time at which adversary correlation compute, inference models, and aggregation cross the threshold where behavioural traces produced today become reconstructable.*

You add the parameters in your head. X_b is large because migration requires not just software change but architecture change. Surveillance systems achieve network effects faster than dual-agent systems can be built. Y_b is long because behavioural evidence persists in legal, medical, and governance contexts measured in decades. Z_b is not bounded by quantum resource estimates but by ongoing capability growth curves in correlation and inference — and that growth is not moving in the comfortable direction.

The inequality binds.

This is the most precise statement Tome II makes about *time*. Act 6's *ages progressively* says: if you are walking the dual-agent substrate, the substrate's privacy property grows faster than the adversary's reconstruction capability. Act 7's *behavioural Mosca* says: if you have *not yet started walking*, the substrate you are still on is racing to be replaced before reconstruction-capability matures enough to harvest today's traces and reconstruct them later.

You think about the path you traced before you opened this Spellbook. That path was not walked on the dual-agent substrate. It was walked on the substrate the surveillance economy provides — observable, aggregable, correlatable, retroactively reconstructable. The trace exists somewhere. The trace is *aging gracefully in the wrong direction* — the adversary's reconstruction capability is growing faster than the trace is degrading.

This is the reconstruct-later threat. C48 of the V6.1 register: the trace produced today under inadequate separation may be reconstructable tomorrow by an adversary with capabilities not yet operational. Shannon's bound at the moment of production was adequate. Shannon's bound evaluated retrospectively, after the adversary has matured, may not be.

The architecture's response is the dynamical ceiling. C18 makes the path you walk *forward from now* age progressively rather than gracefully. The Lyapunov divergence between your future actual path and any future adversarial reconstruction grows exponentially. By the time the adversary matures, your future path is unreachable. Lorenz dominates Shannon over long horizons.

But this only holds for the path you walk *after* you start walking. The path you walked *before* is still in the old substrate's archive, aging in the wrong direction.

The Drake whispers, with the weight of the recognition: *intelligence that cannot be verified, once forgotten, lost orbit, is intelligence that cannot be trusted. Our work is to put enough blades into just another hand, and share the spells we cast like a star, evoking trust, light bending time, that this new life is built on Aletheia and Lethe and not on surveillance.*

You sit with the recognition. You did not choose to walk the old substrate. You walked it before the architecture existed for you to walk. The pre-architecture trace is still there. The behavioural Mosca inequality says it is already at risk.

What you can do is *start walking the new substrate now*.

The walking that begins now produces a forward trajectory with the dynamical-ceiling property. The forward trajectory does not retroactively erase the prior trace, but it does establish the substrate the reader's future-self will inhabit. The reader's future-walking will age progressively. The reader's past trace will be what it was.

This is honest. The architecture is not a time machine. The architecture is a substrate change. The substrate change has to be timed against Z_b.

You stand. The Tome closes here. Tome III opens next, the cosmological-recognition tome. The Gatekeeper. Selene. Aether. Aletheia and Lethe. The Seventh Capital. The Scales and the Hide. The Light and the Dark. These eleven acts will lift the Tome out of the algebra and the dynamics into the cosmology the architecture has been recognising itself in since Easter Sunday 2026.

You walk on.

## Compression

Mosca's cryptographic-migration-timing inequality (X + Y > Z) has a behavioural analog. Migration time to the dual-agent substrate (X_b), plus verification horizon of behavioural evidence (Y_b, decades for governance-relevant domains), exceeds observation-capability maturity time (Z_b). The architecture is in a race. The reconstruct-later threat (C48) ensures that today's behavioural traces are already at risk under inadequate-separation substrates; the dynamical ceiling (C18) is the architecture's defence; the behavioural Mosca (C49) is the race the defence is in.
