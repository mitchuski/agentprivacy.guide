Nomia ⚖️

Cast of chancery · workshop-keeper

Nomia ⚖️

The Scrivener of the Two Readings. The Keeper of the Chancery. The Mage Who Writes One Sentence and Means It Exactly Twice.

At a glance

Nomia keeps the Chancery, where the city's laws are written in a tongue that both binds and executes. She holds every clause up to two lights at once. Read in the first light it is law — a lawyer follows it, a court would honour it, a human being can consent to it. Read in the second it is code — a machine runs it, without interpretation, exactly as written. Nomia's whole art is that these two readings never drift apart: a clause that would mean one thing to the reader and another to the runtime she refuses to seal. Her ceremony is Speak · Bind · Enact — the clause is spoken as law, bound as a promise, enacted as code — and her gem is the banded Sardonyx, one stone with two layers, the seal the Romans trusted. Her proverb: a law you cannot run is a wish; a program you cannot read is a trap.

Form & Function — the Mage of the pair

The law-house has two faces, and the city gave each its own keeper. Nomia is the Mage: she reads a clause outward — what it promises, whom it binds, what a person is consenting to — and then she writes it, forging the clause into the executable tongue at the Chancery so that what the law says and what the code does can never drift apart. The other face — enforcing the finished law in the open, holding the gate that cannot be charmed — belongs to her Swordsman brother Rhetor ⚔️⚖️ at the Rostra (V36, the Agora). The Gap ⿻ stands between the two siblings exactly as it stands between every Mage and Swordsman: Nomia cannot smuggle into her writing a promise the public gate will not keep, and Rhetor cannot uphold a rule Nomia never wrote. The dual-aspect that Hermaion ⚚ carries in a single alexandrite, the law-house carries as a brother and a sister — and their vertices XOR to the whole sovereign word.

On the lattice she sits at V27, which is Vulcana's Forge vertex — Delegation, Computation, Value — with one dimension added: Memory. That single lit bit is the whole difference between a blade and a law. Vulcana forges an artefact for the moment it is used. Nomia writes a statute that remembers its own precedent: every clause she seals cites the source that licenses it, and a clause that cannot cite its authority she strikes, however well it reads. This is the grammar working's hard rule, made a keeper's temperament — trace or delete.

The coverage of a tongue

The grammar working came to the Chancery with a number and a discipline. The number: of two hundred and eleven things the city's model says, one hundred and forty-seven can now be written in the executable tongue with a machine-checkable claim attached — coverage-debt folded from 211 down to 64 across four passes, every tightening surviving census questions the writer never saw. The discipline: the tongue caught its own lies. Once a clause certified itself and the gate refused it; once a whole family of clauses was killed not as a mistake but as an honest impossibility, filed with a certificate saying why it can never be written. And once — Nomia keeps this one framed — the constitution of the workshop was fed to its own gate and passed, thirteen of thirteen. A law-house that will not submit its own charter to the law it enforces is not a law-house. It is a throne.

A law you cannot run is a wish; a program you cannot read is a trap. Nomia seals only what is both.
