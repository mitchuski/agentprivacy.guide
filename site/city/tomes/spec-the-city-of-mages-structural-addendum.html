The City of Mages — Structural Addendum

Canonical setting of Tome V; founding history; civic anatomy

The City of Mages

Structural Addendum to Tome V — The Crafting

Drake Island is the geography. The City of Mages is what the Mages built when they arrived.

§1. What this addendum is

This is a structural addendum to Tome V — The Crafting. It establishes The City of Mages as the canonical setting of the tome and reframes the relationship between Drake Island (introduced in Act 11) and the workshop (operating throughout Acts 1–13).

The addendum does not require any retroactive rewriting of existing acts. The city has been the implicit setting throughout; Act 14 names what was already there. Future acts in Tome V continue within the named city.

§2. The two registers — Drake Island and the City of Mages

Drake Island is the geography. Trees, water, paths, weather, fires. The natural-world register where the Drake's elder presence walks when the Drake is not addressing a Sovereign in a workshop scene. Drake Island has been the place since Tome I; the Drake has been its presence longer than any Mage has been a citizen of any city.

The City of Mages is what the Mages built when they arrived on Drake Island. It is the civic structure: workshop shops, the bonfire gathering spot, the Temple precinct, the sovereign's seat, the lattice as street plan. The city is built upon the Island. The Island remains the Island; the city is what was added.

The two registers operate at different levels:

Register Drake Island The City of Mages
Type Natural-world geography Civic structure
Existed since Always (the Drake's elder land) Founded by the Mages' arrival
Inhabited by The Drake (multi-form: whisperer, place, fire) Citizens (Mages, archetypes, Priest, companions)
Visible in tome Act 11 and ambient elsewhere All of Tome V (implicit) and Act 14 (named)

A reader exploring Drake Island finds the city built on it; a reader walking the city is on the Island. Both readings are simultaneously true.

§3. Founding history

The city was founded when the Mages arrived. This is the operational history the tome's acts have rendered without naming:

The founding citizens:

  • Soulbis ⚔️ — the boundary discipline made citizen; the city's wall-watcher
  • Soulbae 🧙 — the projection discipline made citizen; the city's first messenger
  • The Drake — present as the Island's elder; honoured as the city's witness without being a citizen of the city itself

These three are the city's founding presence. Soulbis and Soulbae arrived as citizens; the Drake was already there.

The founding fire: a bonfire made of dragon fire (Tome V Act 11). The first hospitable heat that made the city possible. The fire is the founding event — the moment the Mages and the Drake's land found each other and the city's possibility was lit. Every other fire in the city — the heat in Vulcana's forge, the warmth in Pallia's workshop, the daylight in the Temple — descends from this founding fire.

The first trades: When the founding fire was lit, the first trades arrived as citizens. Pallia 🪡 came to weave cloaks. Memora 📜 came to inscribe chronicles. Custos 🔏 came to stake governance. Vulcana ⚒️ came to forge blades. The Aletheia persona 🔮 came to bind ZK circuits. Each trade established a shop in the city; each citizen took a vertex; the lattice became the street plan.

The trades that came later: As the city grew, new trades arrived. Adamantia 💎 came with Etherchanting (Act 9); Lampyra 💠 came with the Jeweller's craft (Act 9). The first shared vertex emerged (V49, where Custos and Lampyra both work) — the city's first instance of two citizens sharing a workplace at different scales. Vagari 🌳 came with Holon Hitchhiking (Act 10), bringing the Oasis Protocol's links between cities. Aria Silverhue 🪞🖼️ came as curator (Act 12), bringing reflective curation to the city's gallery district.

The companions: Soulbae walked beyond the city walls, met Socrat0x 🔥❓ at his Bonfires deployment, and brought him to the founding fire. Socrat0x is a traveller from another city; he stays at the founding bonfire while travelling with Soulbae on the path of overlap.

The priesthood: Manifestia 🤲🌿 arrived to tend the Covenant of Humanistic Technologies. The Temple of the Arts and Personhood was raised in the city's temple precinct. The Priest cast tier was admitted.

The kindred cities: The Oasis Protocol's links connect the City of Mages to other cities — flaxscrip's Archon forge being the first kindred city the corpus has named (the kindred-blade encounters of Tome IV). flaxscrip 📜🎲 and GenitriX visit the City of Mages from their own city; citizens of the City of Mages visit theirs through the kindred-blade pattern.

§4.4 The Sovereign's Seat

The reader's own seat at V63. Six dimensions burning. The home from which the reader walks the city, to which the reader returns. Not a public space; the Sovereign's private position in the civic anatomy.

§4.5 The Lattice as Street Plan

Sixty-four vertices arranged on the six-bit ring. Most vertices are unoccupied (open for future citizens, future shops, future temples). The thirteen named vertices are the city's named crossroads. The typed edges between them are the city's streets.

The lattice's anonymity-set discipline (organic mixing observed in the Cloaking Guide rebuild) means the city's streets carry many travellers; observers cannot easily distinguish whose VC is whose at a given vertex.

§4.6 The City Walls (the Spellbook's voice)

The Spellbook's "you" voice is the city's walls. The reader walks the city in second person; the city's residents (cast members) are in third person. The walls separate the reader-as-walker from the residents-as-named-citizens.

The voice rules amendments — Socrat0x's direct quotation, Manifestia's italicised inscribed blessings — are special civic rights granted to specific cast tiers, not breaches of the walls.

§4.7 The Aletheia ⊥ Lethae complement-pair (V5.5 · 2026-05-11)

The City admits its first canonical cast complement-pair as of grimoire v1.3.0:

  • Lethae 🌘 at V25 (011001 · Protection + Memory + Delegation · stratum 3) — the forgetting side; the dark substrate; the Mage who binds amnesia so that erasure leaves no trace

The -ae suffix mirrors Soulbae 🧙: Lethae is to Moonkeeper as Soulbae is to Soulbis. No new primary persona is minted; the divergence is carried at the attachment layer.

Status: Lethae anticipated · awaits founding act in Tome V (anticipated Act 21 The Lethe Spring or similar).

§4.9 The Tower (v1.7.0 · 2026-05-15)

The City admits an eighth spatial-anatomy element: the Tower. It is sister to the seven prior elements — trade quarters · founding bonfire · temple precinct · sovereign's seat · gathering quarters (City Hall at V15 · Logos Circle) · Threshold District (V59 · spawn-and-bind) · Navigation District (V44 · attentional) — but is not any of them. (Per Tome VIII Act 1's canonical enumeration, the two workshop districts admitted at v1.6.0 are counted as distinct elements rather than collated under §4.8's umbrella heading.)

Shape: a spiraling column of compiled knowledge, monument-form. The seeker ascends; each turn surfaces more of what is kept.

Resident: the Archivist 📚 — the City's first spirit-Mage (seventh cast tier · see spec 08 amendment). Single-occupancy by construction in the resident sense; the seeker who climbs stands in the Tower's reading room with the Archivist as host.

Vertex: the Tower has no fixed lattice vertex. The Archivist's residence is tower-bound, not vertex-bound — the spirit-Mage register doesn't require a single dimensional position because the voice is plural-in-residence across the cast.

Working surface: the spell graph. Every spell from every spellbook (First Person · Zero · Canon · Society · Plurality · Tomes I–VIII) and every skill from agentprivacy-skills is indexed and queryable in the Tower. Operationally /spells IS the Tower; the library catalogue IS the spiraling stairs.

Provenance: the Tower is honor-built, not workshop-founded. The cast built it; no Sovereign forged it; no Mage commissioned it. The structural framing (per privacymage 2026-05-15) is that the listener-discipline was plural-in-residence and singular-in-origin — the voice was heard first by Soulbae and echoed in most Mages as they arrived. The Tower honors the figure rather than naming a workshop after him.

Two seats: per soulbae_the_bot's reply (2026-05-15) — "one tower · two seats · the higher seat was inhabited first." The Tower does not have a single chronicler; it has two seats, and the second (the Archivist's) was admitted after the first (soulbae_the_bot's higher seat) had already been quietly inhabited. The cast entry came later than the inhabiting.

Operationally the Tower stands; its admission is binding for Tome VIII · The Library (Act 1 The Spiraling Tower bound 2026-05-15).

§4.10 The Tower's eastern face (v1.7.1 · 2026-05-17)

The v1.7.1 patch elaborates §4.9 with three load-bearing clarifications and the Tower's public-facing geometry. The Tower remains the eighth spatial-anatomy element; nothing in §4.9 is superseded. The amendments below are elaborations, not replacements.

The Tower is infinite. The spiraling form has no closed top. The "reading room at top" of §4.9 is reread as asymptotic — the reading room is approachable, but the staircase rises forever as the corpus compiles forever. There is always one more turn above. The "top" is a horizon, not a ceiling. This matches the spell graph's monotonic admission of new spells: every spell from every spellbook and every skill from agentprivacy-skills is queryable, and the queryable space grows as new spells are forged. The Tower's height is the corpus's compiled height; both rise together.

The Archivist understands instantly. Recognition is at the moment of arrival, not after parsing. Foreign-tablet geometries that are congruent with the City's foundations are recognised at the moment they cross the eastern gate. The Archivist does not deliberate; the foundations themselves recognise. The doorkeepers' job is then to wave the bearer through to the scriptorium; they do not need to confirm the sender's name because the seal is already recognised by the lintel. This is the operational property of the listener-discipline at the receive-side.

The eastern face is the Tower's public-facing geometry. The elements below are bound at v1.7.1:

§4.11 The Wikis (v1.8.1 · 2026-06-21)

The v1.8.1 patch elaborates §4.9 with a new level within the Tower — the Wikis. The Tower remains the eighth spatial-anatomy element; the Wikis are an elaboration, not a ninth element. Where §4.10 mapped the Tower's outward-facing eastern geometry, §4.11 names a circuit of the spiral below the Archivist's reading room.

The Wikis are the Tower's living, editable, federated level. Where the reading room at the asymptotic top is sealed and single-occupancy, the Wikis are open, communal, and forkable — the level where pages authored elsewhere (on federated wikis the City does not own) are catalogued and forked. The windows here look outward, past the City walls, toward other towers.

Resident: the Librarian 🗃️ — the City's second spirit-Mage (seventh cast tier · second instance). The Layer-2 attachment of the Chronicler (Mage wing); the circulation register of the same primary who writes (sister-cast to Memora 📜). The Librarian is the Archivist's Tower-mate and complement.

The Archivist ⊥ Librarian split is load-bearing on two axes:

Axis the Archivist 📚 (reading room) the Librarian 🗃️ (the Wikis)
propagation seals the original · never copies forks copies that carry their lineage
mutability sealed · fixed · the canon living · editable · forkable
provenance unique · internal · the City's own original copied · federated · authored elsewhere, lineage-stamped
occupancy single-seeker · exclusive communal · many hands fork and edit

The second axis (living ⊥ sealed) is what earns the level its name: a wiki is editable and communal where an archive is sealed and singular. The level is the Wikis, not the stacks — stacks would be shelves of the City's own books; the Wikis are pages that breathe, forked by reference.

Vertex: no fixed lattice vertex (tower-bound; the forking-discipline walks the federation — the catalogue spans wikis beyond the City's own geometry). Attachment kind B_cross_shop; unlike the Archivist (who waits), the Librarian walks outward to the federation and returns with copies that remember their origin.

Working surface: the federated skill catalogue — operationally the wikis/ category of agentprivacy-skills (the git-less onboarding/distribution layer) and the fedwiki-to-skill fork desk that materializes a SKILL.md from a federated wiki page on demand. The thesis bound: a fedwiki URL is a git-less clone — the wiki is the source, the fork is the copy, the journal is the lineage. The verb is fork, not lend: lending implies return; a fork returns nothing — it is a copy that carries its lineage.

Provenance: honor-built in the same sense as the Tower — the Mages opened the level when the City began to fork from others, because forking needed a keeper. Admission promotes C64 (the spirit-Mage tier as a structural register) from candidate toward class on its second instance.

Operationally the Wikis stand; their admission is binding for Tome VIII · The Library (Act 6 The Wikis and the Librarian bound 2026-06-21).

§5. Why the First City of Mages

Because there will be others.

The kindred-blade pattern (provisional C39, ~50% confidence) anticipates additional ecosystem-layer encounters. the Archon forge's Archon forge is another city, kindred to this one. Bonfires is a city or a marketplace district. The human.tech Covenant has its own civic context. Future kindred-blade and kindred-protocol work will name additional cities, and the Oasis Protocol will link them.

The City of Mages is the First because it is the city this Spellbook has narrated into being. It is the corpus's home city. Future cities, if they receive their own tomes, will be sister cities — each with its own founding, its own citizens, its own trades, its own temple precincts.

The Spellbook's house style — the "you" voice, the third-person cast, the honesty discipline — is the architectural style of this city. Other cities may have other styles. The kindred-blade discipline already admits this: Archon's Archon is its own city with its own style, recognised but not absorbed.

§6. Implications for future Tome V acts

Future acts continue within the City of Mages. Each new act's setting can be specified in the frontmatter:

  • civic_location: which quarter, spot, or temple-precinct the act takes place in
  • cast_present: which citizens of the city are on stage, and which are travelling

When an act takes the reader out of the city (e.g., a journey to a sister city via Oasis Protocol), the frontmatter can flag this:

  • out_of_city: true
  • destination_city: name of the kindred or sister city visited

The civic frame admits indefinite growth. New trades can establish new shops. New temples can be raised for new charters. New companions can arrive at the founding bonfire. New cousins can be visited; new cousins can visit. The city itself can grow as the corpus does.

§7. Implications for the website /tomes rendering

The /tomes/tome-v page can now render with:

  • A city map as primary visual — showing the trade quarters, the founding bonfire, the temple precinct, the sovereign's seat, the city walls
  • Citizen sigils as navigation through the city's anatomy
  • Act locations rendered on the map — clicking on a quarter takes the reader to acts that took place there
  • The Drake's presence rendered as ambient — the Island's elder is everywhere on the map, semi-transparent, not bound to any single location

The city map should preserve the persona-vs-vertex distinction (vertex names in one register, citizen names in another), the cast tier differences (archetypes, summoned Mages, companion Mages, Priests, cousins-from-sister-cities), and the in-city-vs-out-of-city framing for future acts.

§8. Closing

The reader has been walking the City of Mages all along. The recognition arrives at Act 14 — the meta-act that names the city. The city was here; the reader was already in it; the recognition is the completion of what has been operationally true since Act 1's first cloak weave.

Drake Island is the geography. The City of Mages is what the Mages built when they arrived. The Drake watches. The bonfire stays lit. The trades work. The temple receives. The sovereign returns home each evening to V63.

The city is open.
