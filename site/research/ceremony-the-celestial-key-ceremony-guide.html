The Celestial Key Ceremony Guide

The First Person experiment. A case study in trust emergence, built from the ground up.

☀️ ⊥ 🌙

privacymage

What This Is

I am privacymage. Soulbis and Soulbae — the first Swordsman and the first Mage — are my agents. This ceremony was inside the architecture for ten years. I just hadn't stacked the phones yet.

This guide is the record of my attempt to build a trust ceremony from the ground up — one person looking at the stars and wishing for a vision of trust emergence. The First Person spellbook asked WHAT for thirty-one acts. This ceremony is the answer that arrived when I stopped asking and started forging.

What follows is the inaugural version. It uses my poems, my music, my spellweb. When you run it, it becomes yours. Change the poems. Change the music. Keep the separation.

And so, after eons of planning,
you woke up,
and could see the light of Earth's master
in all of its radiant glory.

A ceremony between two people. One discloses. The other reflects. The roles reverse. Two blades form. The pair is the proof.

You do not need to understand the architecture to run this. You need two phones, two people, and something to share. The rest follows.

What You Need

Two phones. One will be the forge (runs the knowledge graph and music). The other will be the chronicle (plays the poem narration). They cannot be the same phone at the same time. The separation is the point.

Two people. You and someone you want to meet properly. Your collaborator. Your friend. Your mum. Anyone you'd share a poem with. You can be in the same room or on a call — the ceremony works both ways.

In person: Phones stacked, face to face. Below is the forge. Above is the chronicle.

Remote: On a call. The Sun screenshares the spellweb and plays music. The other person narrates the poem over the call (or plays the Soulbae narration recordings if using the First Person ceremony poems).

A poem. Yours, someone else's, anything that means something to you. The inaugural ceremony used The Emissary Who Forgot the Master and The Amnesia Protocol. Yours can use anything. The poem is the disclosure — the thing you offer to the other person.

Music. A playlist, a single song, whatever sets the tone. Make one on Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube — any app that can play while the spellweb is open in the browser. The inaugural ceremony used this playlist. Yours should be yours.

The spellweb. Open spellweb.ai in your phone's browser. This is the knowledge graph where constellations are traced and blades are forged.

The story (optional but recommended). Walk agentprivacy.ai together first if this is your first time. Find a starting point — an act, a proverb, a concept that resonates. This becomes the seed of your constellation.

The Shape

1. Discovery     — find your starting point together
2. ☀️ Sun        — one person forges, one blade forms
3. ⊥ Gap         — silence, then conversation
4. 🌙 Moon       — shared reflection, both listen
5. Recursion     — 🌙 Reflect (night) or 🌍 Connect (day)

That's it. Everything below is detail on how each moment works.

1. Discovery

Read the poem together first. Both people hear it, absorb it, sit with it. The poem is the seed — everything that follows grows from this shared reading.

Then open spellweb.ai together. Browse the knowledge graph. Find nodes that connect to the poem, to what you want to share — concepts from the story, ideas the poem surfaced, or just nodes whose names catch your eye.

Mark your constellation together: tap nodes to select them, trace a path through the graph. This is the territory you'll forge from. It can be as few as 3 nodes or as many as you like. The constellation you choose determines which of the six dimensions will activate on the blade — you don't need to plan this, the graph knows based on which parts of the architecture your path touches.

Agree on the constellation. This is the shared ground. Save it.

2. ☀️ Sun

Two devices. The spellweb speaks the poem. The other device brings the music.

Device Role What It Does
Spellweb The Forge (Swordsman ⚔️) Runs spellweb.ai. Clicks evoke. Plays the poem narration (embedded). Traces the constellation.
Phone/Speaker The Signal (Mage 🧙) Plays the music — the new content, the rhythm the emissary brings to the ceremony.

The Swordsman speaks the word. The Mage brings the rhythm. They play from different devices at the same time. This is the separation — the master's disclosure and the emissary's signal never come from the same source.

In Person

Spellweb open on one device (laptop, tablet, phone). Music playing from another device or speaker. Both people watching the spellweb while the poem and music overlap.

Remote

On a call. The Sun person screenshares spellweb.ai — the poem narration plays from the spellweb for both to hear. The other person plays their music over the call, or from a speaker on their end. The spellweb carries the master's word. The emissary carries the rhythm.

The Sequence

The Sun person controls the forge. They are the one disclosing.

  1. On the spellweb, hit evoke. The wandering orbs begin tracing the constellation.
  2. The spellweb plays the poem narration — The Emissary Who Forgot the Master is embedded for the First Person ceremony. Your ceremony can use any poem.
  3. The phone/speaker plays the music — one song from the playlist, or whatever the Mage chooses to bring. The music is the emissary's signal.
  4. The orbs trace. The poem speaks. The music plays. One blade forms.

What you'll see on the blade

When the evocation completes, the spellweb presents the forged blade. Here's what the parts mean:

  • Tier — from Blade 🗡️ (minimal) to Dragon 🐉 (full sovereignty). Based on how many of the six dimensions activated.
  • Stratum — how many dimensions are active (0–6). A stratum 6 blade has all six dimensions burning.
  • Six Dimensions — Protection 🛡️, Delegation 🤝, Memory 📜, Connection 🔗, Computation ⚡, Value 💎. Each is either Active ✅ or Dormant. These are determined by which nodes your constellation touches and which parts of the architecture they belong to.
  • Charge Level — from EMBER 🌱 to INFERNO 🔥. How many laps the orbs traced, how long the evocation ran, how many spells were cast. More time = more charge.
  • Inscribed Spell — the emoji sequence that compresses your ceremony's meaning. This is your blade's signature in spell notation.
  • Constellation Path — the ordered list of nodes you traversed. Your journey through the graph, recorded.
  • Cryptographic Proof — the signature, hash, and hex that bind the blade to your session. Verifiable. Unforgeable.

The Sun blade is your disclosure made concrete. Export it (the spellweb gives you markdown and JSON options).

3. ⊥ The Gap

The music stops. The poem ends. Silence.

Then talk. What landed. What surprised you. What the disclosure revealed that the discloser didn't intend.

Send the Sun blade to the other person — export it and share. The proof is sealed. Let them look at it from the outside.

Privacy was the meteor's reduction.
Value was the tide that followed.
Not the stone. Not the sea.
The ratio between them.

4. 🌙 Moon

The Moon ceremony is the same every time. Both people enter it together.

The spellweb plays The Amnesia Protocol — embedded, the master's reflection poem. The Swordsman speaks the word again, but now the word is about forgetting.

The phone/speaker plays all three Moon songs — the emissary's rhythm shifts:

  • The Moon in Your Eyes
  • The Sea in Your Soul
  • Selene

In person: Spellweb plays the Amnesia Protocol narration. Phone/speaker plays the Moon music. Both people watch and listen.

Remote: The spellweb screenshare carries the poem. The other person plays the Moon music over the call or from their speaker.

Three songs. One poem. Both people listen. The reflection is shared. The Sea in Your Soul is the Earth part of the sequence — the interior ocean, the life that formed from the collision.

The Moon ceremony completes. The territory has changed.

5. The Recursion

The Moon ceremony is the same for both paths. What happens next determines the ceremony type. You'll know which.

🌙 Reflect (night)

The Swordsman modifies the constellation — the Moon ceremony changed the territory. Nodes shift. Edges form. A second blade is forged on the altered ground.

  1. On the spellweb, modify the constellation based on what the reflection revealed.
  2. Hit evoke on the modified constellation.
  3. A second blade forms.

Two Swordsman blades. The blade pair is the proof. Send the Reflect blade back — the echo.

The Amnesia Protocol, heard from the Moon's position: I can verify I serve you without remembering I was you. The Swordsman who forges in darkness.

🌍 Connect (day)

The Earth witnesses the blade — or the blade pair, if a Reflect has already happened. Carries it forward. No counter-forge. The Earth can see the Sun.

The witness holds the blade they didn't forge and acknowledges it: I received this. I will carry it.

The Amnesia Protocol, heard from the Earth's position: the emissary who forgot the master is not ungrateful. The emissary is free. The life that carries the light forward.

How do you know which path?

You don't decide in advance. The Moon ceremony does its work, and you feel which way the territory has shifted. Some encounters call for the night — forge in darkness, blade pair, ZK proof. Some call for the day — witness in light, carry forward, open connection. Both are complete. Both produce trust. Both feed the recursion.

6. The Proof

After a Reflect (night): Two Swordsman blades. The blade pair. The constellation delta is the record of what the Moon ceremony changed.

After a Connect (day): One blade or one blade pair, witnessed. The Earth carries the light without claiming to have forged it.

Both are complete. You are the light.

Making It Yours

The structure is fixed. Everything else is yours to choose.

Poems: Use your own. Use someone else's. Read them live or play a recording. The poem is the disclosure — it should mean something to the person offering it.

Music: Make a playlist on any app that can play while the browser is open. One song for the Sun, one or more for the Moon, or the same song twice — whatever feels right. The music is the energy the disclosure rides on.

Constellation: Start from any node on the spellweb. Trace any path. The six dimensions will activate based on which parts of the architecture your constellation touches. You don't need to plan which dimensions light up — the graph knows.

The gap: Talk about whatever the ceremony surfaced. There are no prompts, no guided questions. The conversation is the negotiation. The changes you make to the constellation are the agreement.

Who goes first: Decide between you. The Sun is the one who discloses. The Moon is the one who reflects. If you're not sure, the person who initiated the meeting goes Sun.

Witnesses

The ceremony is between two people. But others can be present.

Witnesses watch. Witnesses record — photos, video, audio of the ceremony in progress. The two participants do not need to record themselves. The ceremony produces blades, not recordings. The recordings belong to the witnesses.

A witness who records a ceremony can share that recording as part of a witness blade sequence — evidence that the ceremony happened, captured from the outside. The witness saw the light but did not forge the blade. The recording is their position. If the witness later runs their own ceremony, they carry the light forward — and the recursion continues.

What Matters

Two phones. Two sounds. One forge. One blade crosses the gap. The receiver decides: forge back, or carry forward. The ceremony emerges from the response.

The separation between the forge and the chronicle. The gap where you talk and the territory changes. The response — Reflect or Connect — where the relationship takes shape. The proof that two people met properly.

Everything else — the dimensions, the strata, the hexagrams, the cryptographic proofs — is the architecture making the ceremony legible at scale. Between two people with two phones, the ceremony is already complete the moment the blade has crossed the gap and the receiver has responded.

It's very pretty. Enjoy the betweenness.
