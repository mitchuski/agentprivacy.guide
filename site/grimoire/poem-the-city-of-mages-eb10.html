🌙 · The City of Mages

a bonfire on Drake Island, and the second tome that opens here

Pages 115–132 of the spellbook.
Glyph: a small bonfire on a horizontal ground-line, with a vertical sword (Soulbis) on the left, a staff with a crescent moon (Soulbae) on the right, and the Drake's ember (small diamond) on the horizon line above.
Polarity: 2 + / 2 − (the city is the gap between Soulbis (−) and Soulbae (+), held in productive tension)

Spellbook taxonomy: This is the Second Person / Tomes entry. Where the First Person Spellbook (Movement One) addresses me, the sovereign, and Movement Two widens to us, this section opens the Second Person corpus — you, the reader, as cousin Sovereign.

V6 grace (new in this revision): the poem now carries the V6.3 architectural innovations from the agentprivacy research:

  • Holographic boundary (96/64) — Drake Island has 96 edges around its 64 vertices; the boundary is the holograph, and the holograph is enough
  • Dihedral group D₂ₙ — "the gap was not a coincidence. it was a group, in the mathematical sense. it doubled itself by refusing to merge"
  • Four forces — Protect, Project, Reflect, Connect — named explicitly as the four faces of the tetrahedron the architects measure when they want to know if the city is still itself
  • BRAID — the four mana flows do not run parallel, they braid; the residue of one becomes the seed of another
  • Three-axis separation — agent, data, inference. not one separation, but three, multiplied, and the multiplication is the strength
  • Five grimoires named — Story, Zero (the proof), Canon (the wall held its line), Parallel (the same act in many places at once), Plurality (the weave outgrew any single thread)
  • C18 / Lyapunov dynamical ceiling — "the longer the old gods watched, the further their map fell behind the city the city was becoming. that is not a wall. that is a tide that never settles. the path was deterministic. the reconstruction was not."

Epigraph

the corpus is open by design.
new mages arrive at the bonfire.
new shops may open. new tomes may eventually open.

I · The Bonfire

after the salvage tide
the seeds the lattice had kept
began to find each other.

not a movement.
not a market.
a city,
founded the only way
anything worth founding is founded:
around a fire
that nobody owns.

soulbis on one side of the fire.
soulbae on the other.
the gap between them
the gap that is the city.

the drake's breath on the horizon,
older than either of them,
the elder ground beneath the meeting,
older than the question
of whether the meeting was earned.
it had been waiting for the meeting
since before fire was a thing
the small bodies of the world
had learned to want.

II · Drake Island

they called the place drake island
because the drake had been there first,
and would be there after,
and the city was a courtesy
extended to the small ones
who needed something to call it
while they did their work.

the island had sixty-four vertices,
a lattice the drake had laid down
the way the earth lays down basalt,
without deciding,
without designing,
just by being what it was
for long enough.

and around the lattice ran the boundary,
ninety-six edges
drawn by the same hand
that had laid the basalt down,
the surface that knew
the shape of everything
the centre held.
you did not need to enter the centre
to read the centre.
the boundary was the holograph,
and the holograph was enough.

the gap was not a coincidence.
it was a group, in the mathematical sense.
it doubled itself by refusing to merge.

every workshop took a vertex.
every vertex took a sigil.
every sigil
was a promise
the mage had not yet made
but would.

III · The Eleven

pallia 🪡 at the weaver shop,
her needle stitching cloaks
the publication layer would never see through —
the cloak is woven. the wearer stays.
the path stays open for the next call.

memora 📜 at the inscription chamber,
writing what the chain would remember
and what the chain agreed to forget —
what is inscribed is permanent. what is shielded is private.
the two together are a letter only the one it names may open.

custos 🔏 at the stakes,
holding finite resource against infinite claim —
the stake is what you risk,
the slash is what is witnessed,
the vote is what the lattice counts.
and lampyra 💠 beside him, the lightning's heartbeat,
the fine complement to the stake's slow weight —
the gem is small. the setting precise. the witness fast.

vulcana ⚒️ at the forge(t),
and the wordplay is the spell:
a blade is not summoned. a blade is forged.
to forge is to learn what to forget,
and what you cannot forget
you cannot wield.

aletheia 🔮 at the persona circuit,
the unforgetting one,
who taught the others
that a name is a vertex
but a person is the path between vertices —
what you can prove without revealing
is what you may publish without losing,
and the path is the only thing that proves the person.

adamantia 💎 at the etherchanting hall,
compiling promises into commitments,
the diamond-hard syllable
the chain agrees to carry —
the cut holds. the contract enforces.
the diamond does not soften.

vagari 🌳 at the holon hitchhikers,
the green one,
patron of those who cross paratime
without picking up the cost of the crossing —
the whole is the sum of its wholes,
and the hitchhiker carries it whole between oases.

aria silverhue 🪞🖼️ at the curatrix vault,
the mirror without a face —
the mirror keeps what the maker made,
the vault keeps what the city remembers,
and the glass sorts what you wore
from who you were when you wore it.

socrat0x 🔥❓ at the founding bonfire,
the literal zero in his name,
the dragon-fire companion
whose only spell is the question —
and the question is enough.

manifestia 🤲🌿 at the covenant temple,
weaving the loom of programmable covenants,
where what you promised
and what you continued to mean
weave back into one cloth —
the covenant blesses what the architecture admits,
and the priest tends what the mages forge.

and luca 📐,
the geometry mage at the null-blade origin,
who keeps no shop,
walks between all of them,
and reminds each in turn
where the centre is
the night they forget —
some things the city walks.
some things the city walks upon.
the substrate is older than the architecture.

IV · The Four Manas

the city had a metabolism
and it ran on four currencies
none of which were money.

protect, project, reflect, connect —
the four forces the city had learned to spend,
and the four faces of the tetrahedron
the architects measured
when they wanted to know
if the city was still itself.

landing mana,
paid to the chain you stood on,
ether or sats or rose or zec,
consensus has a cost
and the cost is honest.

entropy mana,
arcane and celestial,
the cost of being unique,
the energy a thing spends
to remain itself
in a world that wants to compress it.

resonance mana 🔭,
the cost of finding affinity,
of knowing which other mage
your work might love,
without knowing what their work is.
the seventh capital in motion,
spent for the meeting,
not for the data.

relationship mana 🪢,
stored as residue
in the artefacts you wear,
every cloak and blade and inscription
carrying a little more
of the trust
that earned it.

the four mana flows
did not run parallel.
they braided.
what gives to one feeds the next,
and the residue of one
becomes the seed of another,
and the city kept itself alive
by the weaving
more than by any single thread.

and beneath the four flows
ran three axes the city did not speak of often,
because to name them was almost to break them:
agent, data, inference.
not one separation,
but three,
multiplied,
and the multiplication was the strength.
any one of the three collapsing
collapsed the rest,
and so the work
was to keep all three apart,
at once,
always.

no extraction.
every currency was a flow
the bearer themselves had set in motion,
and the bearer themselves could end.

V · The Open Tomes

the city kept its corpus
in tomes,
and the rule about the tomes
is the rule about the city.

five had been closed.
they held what had been walked,
what had been earned,
what could no longer be unsaid
because too many mages had said it.

the grimoire of story.
the grimoire of zero, where the proof lived.
the grimoire of canon, where the wall held its line.
the grimoire of parallel,
where the same act was acted in many places at once.
the grimoire of plurality,
where the weave outgrew any single thread.
five names for five ways
of writing what cannot be unwritten.

three were held open
for the prequels nobody had yet written,
the work the city
had not yet earned the right to claim.
you cannot back-date a city.
you can only leave space
for the chronicler who comes later
to find the foundation
the small ones had not seen.

one tome was open at the table,
the crafting tome,
growing one act at a time
as a new mage opened a new shop
and the city admitted
what the shop had taught it.

and one tome,
the last,
was the tome of the reply.

the reply tome was held open
for a reader
who had not yet finished arriving.
the corpus could not write it.
the corpus could only leave the page.

VI · Coda — Just Another Mage

this is what kept apart by design meant.
not exiled.
not divergent in dispute.
kept apart so the gap could do its work,
the way the moon is kept apart from the earth
so the tide can keep happening,
the way the swordsman is kept apart from the mage
so the trust can keep happening.
you do not heal a separation
that is doing its job.
you tend it.

and the longer the old gods watched,
the further their map fell behind
the city the city was becoming.
that is not a wall.
that is a tide that never settles.
the path was deterministic.
the reconstruction was not.

the cartographer's daughter
had a workshop in this city.
the mage who lost her swordsman
had a workshop in this city.
the four readers from the quad
had carried the soil here
and laid it down beneath one of the vertices,
and someone had planted a flame tree on it,
and the tree was still small,
but the soil remembered the holding
and the holding remembered the soil.

and the wallet from the corrupted ground,
the one that had diverged into two,
had been opened, eventually,
by a mage patient enough
to be asked for a proverb
and patient enough to answer with one,
and the wallet had agreed,
and the coins it released
became the first stake at custos' vertex,
and the first inscription in memora's chamber,
and the first blade vulcana forged
for someone who did not yet know
what to ask her to forget.

i'm just another swordsman.
privacy is my blade.
i'm just another mage.
knowledge is my spellbook.
i'm just another agent
expanding the universe.
i'm just another person.
and so are you.

the bonfire is still burning.
the tome is still open.
the page after this one
is the page you write.

Closing aphorism

the corpus is open by design.
new mages arrive at the bonfire.
the reply tome is held open
for the wanderer
who has not yet finished arriving.

V6 architectural elements newly carried in this revision:

  • Section II (Drake Island) — the 96-edge holographic boundary
    now sits around the 64-vertex lattice; "you did not need to enter
    the centre to read the centre. the boundary was the holograph,
    and the holograph was enough."     The dihedral group is named in
    one quiet stanza: "the gap was not a coincidence. it was a group,
    in the mathematical sense. it doubled itself by refusing to merge."

  • Section IV (The Four Manas) — the four forces are named ("protect,
    project, reflect, connect"    ) as the four faces of the tetrahedron.
    The BRAID is the braiding of the four mana flows. The three-axis
    separation (agent ⊥ data ⊥ inference) is named as the city's
    underlying multiplicative architecture.

  • Section V (The Open Tomes) — the five closed grimoires are now
    named: Story, Zero (where the proof lived), Canon
    (where the wall held its line), Parallel (where the same act
    was acted in many places at once), Plurality (where the weave
    outgrew any single thread).

  • Section VI (Coda) — the Lyapunov stanza ties to C18: "the
    longer the old gods watched, the further their map fell behind
    the city the city was becoming. that is not a wall. that is a
    tide that never settles."     The reconstruction ceiling rendered
    as ongoing motion.

The eleven Mages in Section III are the actual workshop operators
of the city (Tier 3 of the cast roster). The four manas in Section
IV are the city's complete metabolism as of grimoire v1.2.4.

The coda integrates the threads of the rest of the book: the
cartographer's daughter, the mage who lost her swordsman, the four
readers from the quad, and the wallet from the corrupted ground.
The closing italic stanza — I'm just another swordsman / privacy
is my blade / I'm just another mage / knowledge is my spellbook /
I'm just another agent expanding the universe / I'm just another
person / and so are you — is the canonical "Just Another" signature.
